# Basma Abdel Aziz

## name
Basma Abdel Aziz / بسمة عبد العزيز

## primary writing language
Arabic

## country / region
Egypt (MENA)

## years active
2007–present

## monumental works
- **الطابور** *Al-Ṭābūr* (*The Queue*) (2013)
    - form: novel
    - Post-uprising Egypt-adjacent city where citizens wait in an ever-lengthening queue for paperwork from "the Gate"; a surveillance-state allegory with Kafkaesque absurdity and sharp medical detail. English tr. Elisabeth Jaquette, Melville House, 2016.
- **هنا بدن** *Hunā Badan* (*Here Is a Body*) (2017)
    - form: novel
    - Street children are abducted and trained as a paramilitary force; explicitly engages post-Arab-Spring Egyptian repression. English tr. Jonathan Wright, Hoopoe/AUC Press, 2021.
- **ذاكرة القهر** *Dhākirat al-Qahr* (*Memory of Repression*) (2014)
    - form: non-fiction / psychological study
    - Non-fiction on political torture; context for her fiction.

## themes
dystopia, power, labor, religion/myth, biology, embodiment

## influences
- Kafka — often acknowledged; *The Queue* was widely compared to *The Trial* on publication.
- Sonallah Ibrahim, Yusuf Idris, and the Egyptian political-realist tradition (she has cited these in interviews).
- Her training as a psychiatrist at the El Nadim Center for Rehabilitation of Victims of Violence in Cairo shapes the medical detail.

## influenced
- Part of the post-2011 Arabic "Egyptian dystopia" wave (alongside Ahmed Naji's *Using Life*, Mohammad Rabie's *Otared*) — though Abdel Aziz's work is the most internationally visible.
- Her translator Elisabeth Jaquette has cited the PEN Translation Prize-winning translation of *The Queue* as a model for contemporary Arabic-to-English literary-SF translation.

## translation history
- **The Queue**: English tr. **Elisabeth Jaquette**, Melville House, 2016. PEN Translation Prize shortlist; Best Translated Book Award longlist 2017.
- **Here Is a Body**: English tr. **Jonathan Wright**, Hoopoe Fiction / AUC Press, 2021.
- German (*Das Gebot*, Unionsverlag 2016, tr. Larissa Bender); French (*La Queue*, Éditions Métailié, tr. Hélène Boisson); Italian (Nottetempo); Spanish, Dutch, Turkish, Portuguese.
- Widely translated following the English 2016 edition.

## notable recognition
- Sawiris Cultural Award (2008, for *Ma wara' al-Tabshir*, non-fiction).
- Named to *Foreign Policy*'s Global Thinkers list (2016).
- PEN Translation Prize shortlist for the English translation (2017).

## brief bio
Basma Abdel Aziz is an Egyptian psychiatrist, writer, and sculptor who has worked for years at the El Nadim Center treating survivors of torture in Cairo. Her non-fiction studies of state repression fed directly into the novelistic imagination of *The Queue* (2013), a dystopia rooted in post-2011 Egyptian bureaucratic-authoritarian reality. Translations from 2016 onward placed her internationally alongside other post-Arab-Spring speculative voices. She has written for *The New York Times* and Egyptian newspapers and is a vocal critic of state torture.
