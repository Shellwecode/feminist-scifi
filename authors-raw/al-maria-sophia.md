# Sophia Al-Maria

## name
Sophia Al-Maria

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Qatar / United States (MENA / diaspora)

## years active
2008–present

## monumental works
- **"The Gaze of Sci-Fi Wahabi"** (2008, essay-blog)
    - form: essay / online
    - Essay-manifesto coining the term **"Gulf Futurism"** for the aesthetic of oil-money, malls, and hyper-constructed Gulf urbanism as SF-in-progress. Co-coined with Fatima Al-Qadiri; widely cited in art criticism.
- **The Girl Who Fell to Earth** (2012)
    - form: memoir
    - Hybrid memoir of growing up between a Bedouin family in Qatar and a rural Washington-state mother; uses SF imagery throughout.
- **Virgin with a Memory** (2014)
    - form: art-book / essay / image
    - Companion piece to her film project on factory workers and the Qatari construction boom.
- **Sad Sack: Collected Writings** (2019)
    - form: essay collection
    - Collects essays, stories, and criticism; includes speculative fiction.

## themes
colonialism, labor, gender, dystopia, ecology, power, embodiment

## influences
- J. G. Ballard — explicitly cited; Ballardian "inner space" is central to Al-Maria's Gulf Futurism.
- Octavia E. Butler — cited.
- Yoshi Sodeoka, the cyberpunk graphic tradition.
- Wahhabi architectural and theological environment — inverted as SF aesthetic.

## influenced
- "Gulf Futurism" has been widely adopted in art-world and academic criticism (Yale, MIT List, Whitechapel exhibitions); it is now a standard reference point in discussions of contemporary Arab speculative aesthetics.
- Her curation (*Black Friday* exhibition at the Whitney Museum, 2016) and video-work have shaped a generation of Gulf-region speculative artists (Monira Al Qadiri, Hasan Hujairi, others).

## translation history
- **The Girl Who Fell to Earth**: Arabic edition (الفتاة التي سقطت على الأرض) tr. details partial; French (*La Fille qui est tombée du ciel*, tr. Coralie Tran Van Hoai, Denoël, 2013); German and Spanish editions.
- Her essays and stories have been anthologised rather than book-translated in most cases.
- **Note**: Al-Maria writes primarily in English and is more an essayist/video-artist than a novelist. Included here because she is the most-cited theorist of Arab/Gulf speculative aesthetics.

## notable recognition
- Whitney Museum solo exhibition *Black Friday* (2016).
- Transmediale Award.
- Film premieres at Tate Modern, MoMA PS1.

## brief bio
Sophia Al-Maria was born in 1983 in Puyallup, Washington, to an American mother and a Qatari Bedouin father, and spent her childhood between rural Washington and Qatar. Her essay "The Gaze of Sci-Fi Wahabi" (2008) and subsequent film and visual-art work theorised "Gulf Futurism" as a way of reading contemporary Arab Gulf urban experience as already-SF. She works in fiction, essay, film, and installation; her writing is included in this dataset as a representative of non-prose-novel but field-shaping speculative work from the MENA region — see the research log for notes on category extension.
