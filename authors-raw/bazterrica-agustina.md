# Agustina Bazterrica

## name
Agustina María Bazterrica

## primary writing language
Spanish

## country / region
Argentina (Latin American)

## years active
2013–present

## monumental works
- **Cadáver exquisito** (*Tender Is the Flesh*) (2017)
    - form: novel
    - A virus has made animal meat toxic; society has legalised and industrialised the consumption of specially bred humans. English tr. Sarah Moses, Pushkin Press (UK) 2020 / Scribner (US) 2020.
- **Las indignas** (*The Unworthy*) (2023)
    - form: novel
    - Post-apocalyptic cult of women inside a Sisterhood; climate-collapsed world. English tr. Sarah Moses, Scribner, 2024.
- **Matar a la niña** (2013)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Early collection.

## themes
dystopia, ecology, gender, biology, religion/myth, embodiment

## influences
- Margaret Atwood's *The Handmaid's Tale* and *Oryx and Crake* have been cited as reference points in interviews, though Bazterrica frames her own book more in the Latin American "boom" and Joyce Carol Oates' horror traditions.
- The Argentine weird-fiction revival (Mariana Enríquez, Samanta Schweblin) is the immediate context.

## influenced
- *Tender Is the Flesh* has become a viral post-2020 bestseller in English translation; along with Schweblin's *Kentukis* and Enríquez's *Our Share of Night*, it anchored the 2020–24 Latin American weird-SF wave in Anglophone markets.

## translation history
- **Tender Is the Flesh**: English tr. **Sarah Moses**, Pushkin Press (UK) / Scribner (US), 2020.
- **The Unworthy**: English tr. **Sarah Moses**, Scribner, 2024.
- French (*Cadavre exquis*, Flammarion), German (*Wie die Schweine*, Suhrkamp 2022, tr. Angelica Ammar), Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, Japanese editions.
- Extensively translated after the 2020 English-language success.

## notable recognition
- Premio Clarín de Novela (Argentina, 2017, for *Cadáver exquisito*).
- Ladbrokes / TikTok-driven bestseller list presence post-2020.

## brief bio
Agustina Bazterrica was born in Buenos Aires in 1974. Her 2017 novel *Cadáver exquisito* won the Premio Clarín, one of Argentina's major literary prizes, and through Sarah Moses's 2020 translation became one of the highest-selling Latin American SF novels ever in English. Her work is graphic, politically specific (factory farming, consumerism, gender violence), and formally spare. She teaches creative writing in Buenos Aires.
