# Lauren Beukes

## name
Lauren Beukes

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
South Africa (African, continental)

## years active
2008–present

## monumental works
- **Moxyland** (2008)
    - form: novel
    - Near-future Cape Town run by corporate sponsorships; four intersecting characters. Debut.
- **Zoo City** (2010)
    - form: novel
    - Johannesburg where criminals are bonded with animal familiars. Arthur C. Clarke Award winner (2011).
- **The Shining Girls** (2013)
    - form: novel
    - A Depression-era Chicago house lets a killer hunt women across decades. Adapted as an Apple TV+ series (2022).
- **Afterland** (2020)
    - form: novel
    - Pandemic kills 99% of men; a mother flees the US with her son, who must be hidden. Published just before and interpreted through the COVID-19 pandemic.

## themes
gender, cyberpunk, power, colonialism, weird/horror, dystopia

## influences
- William Gibson (cited in *Moxyland* interviews).
- Octavia E. Butler — acknowledged.
- The South African post-apartheid urban-fiction scene (Ivan Vladislavić, others).

## influenced
- First African writer to win the Arthur C. Clarke Award (2011, *Zoo City*) — a visibility event for African SF in the English-language market.
- Her success helped open routes for later African SFF writers in the US/UK trade market.

## translation history
- **Zoo City**: French (*Zoo City*, tr. Laurent Philibert-Caillat, Presses de la Cité, 2011); German (*Zoo City*, tr. Rainer Schumacher, Rowohlt); Japanese (*ズー・シティ*, tr. 細美遙子 Hosomi Yōko, 2012, Tokyo Sogensha); Italian, Spanish, Polish, Russian, Czech.
- **Moxyland**: French, German, Polish.
- **The Shining Girls**: widely translated — French (tr. Laurent Philibert-Caillat), German, Spanish, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Turkish, Chinese.

## notable recognition
- Arthur C. Clarke Award (2011, *Zoo City*).
- University of Johannesburg Prize, Strand Critics Award.
- British Fantasy Award.
- CNA Literary Award (South Africa).

## brief bio
Lauren Beukes is a Cape Town journalist turned novelist whose work is consistently located in African, American, and online-global spaces. Her 2011 Clarke Award for *Zoo City* made her the first African writer to win that prize. She works in multiple media: novels, comic-book writing (DC's *Survivors' Club*, *Fairest*), and television (showrunner / adaptation work). Her fiction runs on propulsive genre plots with sharp economic and gendered edges. She lives in Cape Town.
