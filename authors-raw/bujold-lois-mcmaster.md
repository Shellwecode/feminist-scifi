# Lois McMaster Bujold

## name
Lois McMaster Bujold

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
1985–present

## monumental works
- **Shards of Honor** (1986) / **Barrayar** (1991)
    - form: novel / novel
    - Opening of the Vorkosigan Saga; Cordelia Naismith negotiates war, occupation, and a very pregnant escape.
- **The Vor Game** (1990)
    - form: novel
    - Early Miles Vorkosigan novel; Hugo winner.
- **Mirror Dance** (1994)
    - form: novel
    - Cloned-brother novel; Hugo winner.
- **Memory** (1996)
    - form: novel
    - Widely considered the central pivot of the Vorkosigan series; Miles reshapes his career after disability.

## themes
family/kinship, embodiment, power, biology, gender, space-opera

## influences
- Her father, engineer Robert Charles McMaster, and welding-engineering environment (she's cited this repeatedly in interviews).
- Andre Norton (direct, repeatedly acknowledged childhood influence; Bujold has spoken about Norton in Locus interviews and convention talks).
- Georgette Heyer — openly influential on the comedy-of-manners register of her novels.
- Cordwainer Smith — cited in interviews.

## influenced
- Ann Leckie has cited Bujold as a direct influence (acknowledged in Leckie's own blog and in interviews after *Ancillary Justice*).
- Becky Chambers' character-focused, found-family space SF draws on Bujold; Chambers has acknowledged this in interviews.

## translation history
- Vorkosigan novels translated widely: Russian (AST and Eksmo, multiple translators), French (J'ai Lu, various translators), German (Heyne), Polish (Prószyński / Zysk), Japanese (Tokyo Sogensha, tr. 小木曽絢子 Ogiso Ayako and others), Czech, Italian, Bulgarian.
- Specific translator/year chains are documented partially on ISFDB; the Russian editions in particular are multiple and well-documented in Russian fandom sources.

## notable recognition
- Four Hugo Awards for Best Novel (1991, 1992, 1995, 2004), a record matched only by Heinlein at the time she tied it.
- Multiple Nebulas and Locus Awards.
- SFWA Damon Knight Grand Master (2020).

## brief bio
Lois McMaster Bujold began publishing in her mid-thirties, selling her first three novels to Baen in 1985. The Vorkosigan Saga — now some 16 novels and novellas — follows disabled genius Miles Vorkosigan and his family across multiple decades and a politically complex galaxy. Her four Hugo wins for Best Novel put her among a very short list of most-decorated novelists in the genre. Her later *Penric & Desdemona* fantasy novellas have quietly become one of the most consistently rewarded ongoing novella series of the 2010s–20s. She lives in Minneapolis.
