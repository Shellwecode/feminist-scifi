# Priya Sarukkai Chabria

## name
Priya Sarukkai Chabria

## primary writing language
English (also publishes poetry with Sanskrit engagement)

## country / region
India (South Asian)

## years active
1986–present

## monumental works
- **Generation 14** (2008; reissued as *Clone*, 2018, by Zubaan-Hachette)
    - form: novel
    - In the 24th century, Clone 14/54/G is scheduled for "dis-assembly" when she begins recovering forbidden memories from her original-human ancestor. A quiet, literary Indian-English SF novel.
- **Not Springtime Yet** (2008)
    - form: poetry collection
    - Speculative poetics; Muse India award.
- **Bombay/Mumbai: Immersions** (2013)
    - form: essay/prose
    - Hybrid form; speculative-documentary.
- **Sing of Life: Revisioning Tagore's Gitanjali** (2021)
    - form: poetry/translation
    - Reframings of Tagore; not SF but representative of her literary breadth.

## themes
AI/machine-consciousness, time, biology, gender, religion/myth, embodiment

## influences
- Sanskrit poetic tradition (she has translated 9th-century devotional poet Āṇḍāḷ into English).
- Jorge Luis Borges, Italo Calvino — cited.
- Indian classical dance (she was trained in Bharatanatyam) — acknowledged in essays about her embodied imagination.

## influenced
- As founder-editor of *Poetry at Sangam* (online journal, 2013–2023), she has been a major curator of Indian and South Asian poetry translation, influencing an extended community of poet-translators.
- *Generation 14 / Clone* has been cited as a foundational Indian-English SF novel in recent Indian-English SFF anthologies.

## translation history
- **Generation 14 / Clone**: primarily English. Hindi and Marathi translations have been reported (by smaller presses); specific translator attributions are partial.
- **Her Anthology work**: Chabria's Āṇḍāḷ translations (*Autobiography of a Goddess*, Zubaan/Univ. of Chicago, 2015/2016) are translator-visible: Priya Sarukkai Chabria and Ravi Shankar.
- European-language translations of her SF novel appear limited; flagged.

## notable recognition
- Indian Council for Cultural Relations grant.
- Muse India Young Writer Award (earlier career).
- Sahitya Akademi-adjacent visibility.
- Featured in Bodleian Library exhibitions of contemporary Indian-English literature.

## brief bio
Priya Sarukkai Chabria is a Chennai-based Indian poet, translator, and novelist. She trained in Bharatanatyam dance and has a background that bridges classical Indian aesthetics and contemporary speculative writing. *Generation 14* (2008, reissued as *Clone*, 2018) is one of the comparatively few Indian-English-language science fiction novels by a woman from the 2000s; it has been cited in anthology introductions and postcolonial-SF criticism. Her broader work — translating 9th-century Sanskrit devotional poetry, editing *Poetry at Sangam* — situates her SF within a longer Indian literary tradition rather than within Anglo-American genre markets.
