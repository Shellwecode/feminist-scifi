# Becky Chambers

## name
Becky Chambers

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
2014–present

## monumental works
- **The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet** (2014 self-pub; 2015 Hodder)
    - form: novel
    - Found-family starship crew crosses the galaxy to tunnel wormhole pathways. First of the Wayfarers series.
- **A Closed and Common Orbit** (2016)
    - form: novel
    - Follows a shipboard AI downloaded illegally into a bodykit, and a rescued clone child. Hugo winner (Best Series, 2019, for the Wayfarers).
- **A Psalm for the Wild-Built** (2021) / **A Prayer for the Crown-Shy** (2022) — Monk & Robot novellas
    - form: novella duology
    - Solarpunk far-future where robots long ago left humanity; a tea-monk meets a robot that has come back. Hugo winner.

## themes
family/kinship, AI/machine-consciousness, utopia, ecology, gender, first-contact

## influences
- Ursula K. Le Guin (Chambers has cited Le Guin in multiple interviews, specifically *The Left Hand of Darkness* and *The Dispossessed*).
- Lois McMaster Bujold — character-warmth lineage, acknowledged in interviews.
- *Firefly* / Joss Whedon-era TV found-family SF — openly discussed as an aesthetic reference.

## influenced
- A recognizable "cosy SF" or "hopepunk" current in 2010s–20s publishing has been routinely traced to Chambers by reviewers and to the Wayfarers crowdfunding success (Kickstarter, 2012).
- Explicitly invoked as a model by other 2020s hopepunk and solarpunk writers.

## translation history
- Wayfarers series: French (Atalante, *L'Espace d'un an* etc., tr. Marie Surgers); German (*Der lange Weg zu einem kleinen zornigen Planeten*, tr. Karin Will, Fischer Tor, 2016); Japanese (*銀河核へ、ようこそ*, tr. 細美遙子 Hosomi Yōko, 2019, Tokyo Sogensha); Spanish (Insólita), Italian (Fanucci), Polish, Czech, Chinese.
- Monk & Robot: widely translated; German and French editions very quickly after release.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award for Best Series (2019, Wayfarers).
- Hugo Award for Best Novella (2022, *A Psalm for the Wild-Built*).
- Arthur C. Clarke Award shortlist.
- Prix Julia-Verlanger (France, 2017).

## brief bio
Becky Chambers grew up in Southern California in a family of astrobiologists and space educators. She crowdfunded her debut novel through Kickstarter in 2012 after traditional publishers passed; *The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet* became a quiet bestseller when Hodder picked it up in 2015. Her work has been taken as the definitive aesthetic of 2010s "hopepunk" and cosy SF, focusing on social infrastructure, kindness, and non-human personhood rather than conflict plots. She lives in Northern California with her wife.
