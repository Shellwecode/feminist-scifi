# Andrea Chapela

## name
Andrea Chapela

## primary writing language
Spanish

## country / region
Mexico (Latin American)

## years active
2009–present

## monumental works
- **Ansibles, perfiladores y otras máquinas de ingenio** (*Ansibles, Profilers and Other Machines of Wit*) (2020)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Five stories of near-future cognition, AI-mediated love, and social-media selfhood; won the Premio Nacional de Literatura Gilberto Owen 2020.
- **El Verbo J** (2011–2014)
    - form: fantasy tetralogy
    - YA fantasy quartet; her early book-length work.
- **Hermanas del tiempo** / "Sisters of Time" (stories)
    - form: short stories
    - Time-travel and memory SF.

## themes
AI/machine-consciousness, gender, embodiment, time, language

## influences
- Ursula K. Le Guin (cited in interviews and essays; Chapela has written about Le Guin's ansible as a literary symbol).
- Ted Chiang (cited as a short-fiction model).
- The Mexican literary-fantastic and the "Cúmulo de Tesla" peer group.

## influenced
- Part of the 2010s–20s Mexican SF short-fiction community; her Premio Nacional Gilberto Owen win in 2020 raised the public visibility of younger Mexican feminist SF.

## translation history
- Individual stories translated into English in *Latin American Literature Today*, *Samovar* (Strange Horizons' translation magazine), and the *Speculative Latin America* issue of various venues.
- "Pixels" and "Deconstruction" translated into English (translators include Emma Törzs and Adrian Demopulos — exact attribution varies by anthology).
- No English-language book-length collection as of the mid-2020s — flag.

## notable recognition
- Premio Nacional de Literatura Gilberto Owen 2020 (for *Ansibles, perfiladores…*).
- Named to the *Bogotá39* list 2017.
- Young-writer visibility as both fiction writer and literary translator (into Spanish).

## brief bio
Andrea Chapela was born in Mexico City in 1990, studied chemistry at the UNAM, and has lived in Iowa (Iowa Writers' Workshop) and in Tokyo. Her 2020 collection *Ansibles, perfiladores y otras máquinas de ingenio* won the Gilberto Owen Prize and positioned her as a central voice of contemporary Mexican speculative fiction. She writes fiction, essays, and literary translation (into Spanish). English-language availability remains piecemeal through magazine-length translations.
