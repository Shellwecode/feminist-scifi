# Daína Chaviano

## name
Daína Chaviano

## primary writing language
Spanish

## country / region
Cuba (Latin American); based in the United States since 1991

## years active
1979–present

## monumental works
- **Los mundos que amo** (1979)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Winner of the first David Prize for SF in Cuba (1979); foundational to the Cuban SF tradition.
- **Fábulas de una abuela extraterrestre** (1988)
    - form: novel
    - Matriarchal alien society and intergenerational memory; often cited as a central feminist-SF work in Spanish.
- **El hombre, la hembra y el hambre** ("The Man, the Woman and the Hunger") (1998)
    - form: novel
    - Magical-realist novel of Havana's Special Period, with speculative framing. Azorín Prize.
- **La isla de los amores infinitos** (*The Island of Eternal Love*) (2006)
    - form: novel
    - Historical-fantastic saga of Havana.

## themes
gender, family/kinship, religion/myth, utopia, time, colonialism

## influences
- Ursula K. Le Guin (Chaviano has cited in interviews as a central reference).
- The Cuban literary-fantastic tradition: Alejo Carpentier, Lydia Cabrera.

## influenced
- As the founder (1979) of the first Cuban SF workshop, and through the David Prize and her own subsequent Havana-based teaching, Chaviano is credited with shaping the first generation of Cuban SF writers.
- Called "the Queen of Cuban Science Fiction" in Spanish-language criticism.

## translation history
- **The Island of Eternal Love**: English tr. Andrea G. Labinger, Riverhead, 2008.
- **Fábulas de una abuela extraterrestre**: partial English translations in anthologies; **no known complete English edition** — flag.
- French, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Polish, Croatian, Hebrew editions of various novels.
- Translator attribution strongest for the mainstream *Island of Eternal Love* and its sister novels in "La Habana oculta" cycle.

## notable recognition
- David Prize (Cuba, 1979, first SF recipient).
- Anna Seghers Prize (Germany, 1990) — Chaviano was its first Spanish-language recipient.
- Azorín Prize (Spain, 1998).
- Gold Medal, Florida Book Awards (2008).
- Goliardos International Prize for SF, Mexico (2003).

## brief bio
Daína Chaviano was born in Havana in 1957 and founded Cuba's first SF literary workshop in 1979. She emigrated to Miami in 1991. Her work bridges SF, fantasy, and the Cuban magical-realist tradition, and she is regularly cited as the most important Cuban woman SF writer of the twentieth century. Her four-novel cycle *La Habana oculta* (begun 1998) weaves speculative and historical Havana together. Her influence on the Caribbean Spanish-language fantastic is widely acknowledged, though much of her work remains inaccessible in English.
