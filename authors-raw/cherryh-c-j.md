# C. J. Cherryh

## name
Carolyn Janice Cherryh (legal name Carolyn Janice Cherry)

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
1976–present

## monumental works
- **Downbelow Station** (1981)
    - form: novel
    - Space station besieged by the warring Company / Union factions; foundation of the Alliance-Union future history. Hugo winner.
- **Cyteen** (1988)
    - form: novel
    - Cloned replacement of a murdered scientist on an azi-labor-based colony; hard biopolitical SF. Hugo winner.
- **Foreigner** (1994, first of an ongoing series)
    - form: novel series (20+ volumes, ongoing)
    - Human translator on a planet of psychologically non-human "atevi"; sustained first-contact anthropology.
- **The Pride of Chanur** (1981)
    - form: novel
    - Compsny-era novel told from the POV of a matriarchal feline-alien trader captain.

## themes
first-contact, power, labor, language, space-opera, colonialism

## influences
- Classical antiquity (Cherryh holds an MA in Classics and taught Latin); Thucydides and archaic Greek history have been cited in her interviews as structural influences.
- Andre Norton (self-reported — Cherryh wrote several novels in Norton's Witch World setting).

## influenced
- A generation of hard-SF-with-anthropology writers; Lois McMaster Bujold has cited Cherryh as a contemporary reference.
- Jo Walton's essays for Tor.com discuss Cherryh's novels as foundational for modern "competent protagonist under institutional pressure" SF.

## translation history
- *Downbelow Station*: French (*La Station Pell*, tr. Guy Abadia, 1983, Denoël); German (*Pells Stern*, Heyne); Japanese (Hayakawa editions).
- *Cyteen*: French (tr. Laurence Le Maire, J'ai Lu); German (Heyne); Japanese; specific translator/year records partial for some volumes.
- The Foreigner series has been translated selectively — not every volume exists in every language.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Awards for *Downbelow Station* (1982) and *Cyteen* (1989).
- John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer (1977).
- SFWA Grand Master (2016).
- An asteroid (77185 Cherryh) is named for her.

## brief bio
C. J. Cherryh studied Classics at the University of Oklahoma and Johns Hopkins and taught Latin in Oklahoma public schools before her 1976 debut. Her Alliance-Union future history spans dozens of novels and arguably constitutes the most sustained politically realistic space opera in the genre. *Cyteen* (1988) used cloning as the engine of an extended inquiry into consent, class, and identity. The Foreigner sequence, begun in 1994 and still continuing, is one of the longest-running first-contact series in SF. She was the third woman to be named SFWA Grand Master. She lives in Washington state with her wife, artist Jane S. Fancher.
