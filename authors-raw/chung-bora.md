# Bora Chung

## name
Bora Chung / 정보라

## primary writing language
Korean

## country / region
South Korea (East Asian)

## years active
1998–present

## monumental works
- **『저주토끼』 *Jeojuttokki* (*Cursed Bunny*) (2017)**
    - form: short-story collection
    - Ten dark, weird-horror and SF-adjacent stories. English: *Cursed Bunny*, tr. Anton Hur, Honford Star (UK) 2021 / Algonquin (US) 2022. International Booker Prize shortlist (2022); National Book Award (Translated Literature) shortlist (2022).
- **『붉은 칼』 *Bulgeun Kal* (*Red Sword*) (2019)**
    - form: novel
    - Mythic/SF novel.
- **『저주받은 자들의 노래』 *Jeojubadeunjadeurui Norae* (*Song of the Cursed*)** (various editions)
    - form: short-story collection

## themes
weird/horror, gender, labor, biology, religion/myth

## influences
- Bruno Schulz and the Eastern European grotesque (Chung has a PhD in Slavic literature from Indiana University and translates from Russian and Polish).
- Stanisław Lem (whom she has translated into Korean; see translation history).
- Boris Strugatsky (whom she has also translated).

## influenced
- Along with Kim Bo-Young and Kim Choyeop, one of the three writers most associated with the 2020s international boom in Korean SF/weird fiction.
- Her Booker International shortlist (2022) placed Korean weird-horror into the Anglophone literary-prize conversation for the first time.

## translation history
- **Cursed Bunny**: English tr. **Anton Hur**, Honford Star 2021 / Algonquin Books 2022. Booker International shortlist 2022, NBA Translated Literature shortlist 2022.
- **Your Utopia** (2024): English tr. **Anton Hur**, Algonquin — second English collection.
- French (*Lapin maudit*, tr. Choi Kyungran & Pierre Bisiou, Decrescenzo), German (*Der Fluch des Hasen*, tr. Kyong-Hae Flügel, Aufbau 2022), Spanish, Dutch, Italian, Portuguese, Polish, Czech, Japanese — widely translated post-Booker.
- **As translator**: Bora Chung has translated Stanisław Lem (*Solaris* and others) and Boris and Arkady Strugatsky into Korean.

## notable recognition
- International Booker Prize shortlist (2022, *Cursed Bunny*).
- National Book Award for Translated Literature shortlist (2022).
- Korean SF Award.

## brief bio
Bora Chung was born in Seoul, earned degrees in Russian and East European studies (Yale, Indiana), and teaches at Yonsei University. She has translated Lem and the Strugatsky brothers into Korean — a translation lineage that places her inside the European weird-SF tradition as well as the Korean feminist one. *Cursed Bunny*'s international success in 2021–22 made her, along with translator Anton Hur, a central figure in the 2020s Korean literary export boom. She is a union activist in the Korean translators' and writers' labour movements.
