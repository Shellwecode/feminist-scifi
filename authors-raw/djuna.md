# Djuna

## name
Djuna / 듀나 (pen name; real identity deliberately undisclosed)

## primary writing language
Korean

## country / region
South Korea (East Asian)

## years active
1992–present

## monumental works
- **『면세구역』 *Myeonse Guyeok* (*Duty-Free Zone*) (2000)**
    - form: short-story collection
    - Early collection of Korean SF short fiction mixing cyberpunk, horror, and critique of globalisation.
- **『제저벨』 *Jezebel* (2012)**
    - form: novel
    - Colonial planet-world novel; Djuna's "Linker" universe.
- **『평형추』 *Pyeonghyeongchu* (*Counterweight*) (2021; English 2023)**
    - form: novel
    - Corporate-espionage SF on a Pacific island where a space elevator is being built. English tr. Anton Hur.
- **『민트의 세계』 *Minteu-ui Segye* (*Mint's World*)** (various anthologies, 2010s)
    - form: linked stories

## themes
cyberpunk, colonialism, power, AI/machine-consciousness, first-contact

## influences
- Documented self-report is minimal because Djuna maintains pseudonymity and rarely gives interviews. Critical commentary (Park Sang-joon and others) places Djuna in a lineage aware of Le Guin, Philip K. Dick, and Stanisław Lem.

## influenced
- Nearly every younger Korean SF writer of the 2010s–20s names Djuna as a foundational figure; Kim Bo-Young, Kim Choyeop, and others have cited Djuna in interviews and anthology introductions.
- Because Djuna's identity is withheld, the "Djuna effect" in Korean SF is often described in collective terms: an available template for domestic SF independent of translated Anglo-American models.

## translation history
- **Counterweight**: English tr. **Anton Hur**, Pantheon, 2023. Djuna's first full novel in English.
- Short stories: "The Bloody Battle of Broccoli Plain" (tr. details partial) appeared in English anthologies including *Readymade Bodhisattva: The Kaya Anthology of South Korean Science Fiction* (ed. Sunyoung Park and Sang Joon Park, Kaya Press, 2019), with multiple stories by Djuna translated by Sung Ryu, Gord Sellar, and others.
- French: selected stories in Korean-SF anthologies (Decrescenzo).
- Japanese: individual stories in anthologies; translator records partial.

## notable recognition
- Multiple recipient of Korean SF awards (SF Awards-Korea).
- Adapted for film: ***The Bloody Battle of Broccoli Plain*** and **"The End of the World"** were sources for Jang Joon-hwan's 2003 film *Save the Green Planet!* (partial adaptation, as reported in Korean press — confirm).

## brief bio
Djuna is the pseudonym of a Korean SF writer and film critic active since 1992. The identity has never been publicly confirmed, though occasional interviews conducted by email or via intermediaries have been published. Djuna has authored dozens of short stories, essays, and novels, and has been one of the two or three figures credited with creating a distinctly Korean SF tradition. The choice of pen name and continued anonymity are themselves part of the literary-political position Djuna has taken. Anton Hur's 2023 translation of *Counterweight* brought Djuna to a large Anglophone audience for the first time.
