# Angélica Gorodischer

## name
Angélica Beatriz del Rosario Arcal de Gorodischer

## primary writing language
Spanish

## country / region
Argentina (Latin American)

## years active
1964–2022

## monumental works
- **Kalpa Imperial** (1983 and 1984, two volumes)
    - form: linked short-story novel
    - The history of "the greatest empire that never existed," told by rotating bards; an anti-epic epic. English tr. Ursula K. Le Guin, Small Beer Press, 2003.
- **Trafalgar** (1979)
    - form: linked short-story collection
    - Commercial traveller Trafalgar Medrano visits impossible worlds on business. English tr. Amalia Gladhart, Small Beer Press, 2013.
- **Bajo las jubeas en flor** (1973)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Early SF collection; won the Poblet Prize.
- **Prodigios** (1994)
    - form: novel
    - Literary-fantastic novel.

## themes
utopia, power, time, language, colonialism, gender

## influences
- Borges (Gorodischer repeatedly cited Borges as both model and target, in interviews and essays).
- Italo Calvino (structural kinship with *Invisible Cities* often noted by critics; Gorodischer has acknowledged the comparison).

## influenced
- Ursula K. Le Guin discovered Gorodischer through a friend's recommendation, learned Spanish partly to read her, and translated *Kalpa Imperial* in 2003. Le Guin has stated this explicitly in her introduction to the Small Beer edition — "the first American English translation of Angélica Gorodischer's work."
- A generation of Spanish-language fantasists (Agustín Fernández Mallo, Teresa P. Mira de Echeverría) have cited her.

## translation history
- **Kalpa Imperial**: English tr. **Ursula K. Le Guin**, Small Beer Press, 2003. Publisher statement and Le Guin's introduction confirm dates.
- **Trafalgar**: English tr. **Amalia Gladhart**, Small Beer Press, 2013.
- **Prodigios**: English tr. Sue Burke, Aqueduct Press, 2015.
- French: multiple volumes (Actes Sud).
- Italian, German, Portuguese, Russian editions.

## notable recognition
- World Fantasy Life Achievement Award (2011).
- ILCH Dignity Award (Chile, 2007).
- Gilberto Owen National Literature Prize (Mexico, 1996).
- Esteban Echeverría Prize (Argentina, 2007).
- Honorary Doctor from the University of Rosario (2017).

## brief bio
Angélica Gorodischer was born in Buenos Aires, raised in Rosario, and published her first book in 1964. She wrote across SF, fantasy, detective, and feminist literary fiction in Spanish over nearly sixty years. Her work was widely known in the Spanish-speaking world long before it reached English; Le Guin's translation of *Kalpa Imperial* in 2003 introduced her to the Anglophone SF audience. Gorodischer was also a feminist activist; her essays on gender-based violence were collected in *Mujeres de palabra* (2004). She died in Rosario in 2022.
