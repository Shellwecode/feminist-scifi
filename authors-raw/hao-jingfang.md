# Hao Jingfang

## name
Hao Jingfang / 郝景芳

## primary writing language
Chinese (Mandarin)

## country / region
China (East Asian)

## years active
2006–present

## monumental works
- **「北京折叠」 ("Folding Beijing") (2012)**
    - form: novelette
    - Beijing is physically partitioned into three economic zones that share a single geographical space via rotating foldings; a migrant worker crosses strata. Hugo Award for Best Novelette (2016), making Hao the second Chinese writer to win a Hugo after Liu Cixin, and the first Chinese woman.
- **『流浪苍穹』 *Liúlàng Cāngqióng* (*Vagabonds*) (2016)**
    - form: novel
    - Mars and Earth are politically and socially estranged; a group of Martian-born youth who spent five years on Earth return home and cannot reconcile the two societies. English: *Vagabonds*, tr. Ken Liu, Saga Press, 2020.
- **『孤独深处』 *Gūdú Shēnchù* (*Near the Loneliness*)** (2016) collection
    - form: short-story collection
    - Includes "Folding Beijing."
- **「人之彼岸」 ("The Other Side of Humanity") (2017)**
    - form: novella collection on AI
    - Explicit near-future AI speculation, partly informed by her economic-research background.

## themes
dystopia, power, labor, AI/machine-consciousness, utopia, time

## influences
- Italo Calvino (she has cited *Invisible Cities* explicitly as a structural model for "Folding Beijing").
- Ursula K. Le Guin (cited in interviews, particularly for *The Dispossessed* and *Vagabonds*).
- Liu Cixin as the establishing context for 2010s Chinese SF publishing, though Hao's work is stylistically distinct.

## influenced
- One of the central figures of the 2010s "new wave" of Chinese women SF writers alongside Xia Jia, Chen Qiufan, Tang Fei, Gu Shi (though Chen is male; Chinese new-wave is gender-mixed).
- Her Hugo win opened foreign-rights markets for other Chinese SF writers; Ken Liu's translation relationship with Hao is well-documented.

## translation history
- **"Folding Beijing"**: English tr. **Ken Liu**, *Uncanny Magazine* (Jan/Feb 2015); Hugo win 2016.
- **Vagabonds** (novel): English tr. **Ken Liu**, Saga Press, 2020.
- German (Rowohlt), French (Michel Lafon / Actes Sud), Italian (Mondadori), Spanish, Polish, Czech editions of the novella and novel.
- Korean edition.
- "Folding Beijing" is one of the most widely translated Chinese SF works of the 2010s.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award for Best Novelette (2016, "Folding Beijing").
- Yinhe Award (Chinese Galaxy Award).
- Xingyun (Nebula) Award (Chinese Nebula).

## brief bio
Hao Jingfang was born in Tianjin in 1984, holds a PhD in economics from Tsinghua University, and works in economic research at the China Development Research Foundation. Her fiction sits pointedly at the intersection of speculative form and empirical development-economics concerns: housing stratification in "Folding Beijing," inter-planetary political economy in *Vagabonds*. She founded "Tongxing Children" (童行), an educational NGO, and has kept her SF career partial to her research career.
