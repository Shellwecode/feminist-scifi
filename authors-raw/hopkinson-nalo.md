# Nalo Hopkinson

## name
Nalo Hopkinson

## primary writing language
English (with extensive Caribbean Creole and Jamaican Patois usage)

## country / region
Jamaica / Canada / United States (African/Caribbean diaspora)

## years active
1997–present

## monumental works
- **Brown Girl in the Ring** (1998)
    - form: novel
    - Debut novel; near-future collapsed Toronto ruled by gangs, Caribbean-descended young woman learns Obeah to survive. Warner Aspect First Novel Contest winner.
- **Midnight Robber** (2000)
    - form: novel
    - Caribbean-futurist SF: carnival-inflected planetary-colony world and its prison-world twin. Locus-nominated.
- **The Salt Roads** (2003)
    - form: novel
    - Three women connected by a goddess across time: 18th-century Haiti, Alexandria, and 19th-century Paris.
- **Sister Mine** (2013)
    - form: novel
    - Two conjoined Caribbean-Canadian sisters with divided magical and mundane gifts.

## themes
race, gender, religion/myth, language, power, colonialism, family/kinship

## influences
- Octavia E. Butler — direct and much-cited (Hopkinson spoke at Butler's memorial and has written about Butler's mentorship).
- Samuel R. Delany — acknowledged.
- Derek Walcott and the Caribbean literary tradition.
- Caribbean folklore, Afro-Caribbean religious practice.

## influenced
- Edited *So Long Been Dreaming: Postcolonial Science Fiction and Fantasy* (2004, with Uppinder Mehan) — foundational anthology of postcolonial SF.
- Her mentorship and teaching at UC Riverside have influenced a generation of Black, Caribbean, and Indigenous SFF writers; Tochi Onyebuchi, Rivers Solomon, and others have cited her.

## translation history
- **Brown Girl in the Ring**: French (*La Ronde des esprits*, tr. Mathilde Fontanet, Denoël / 2006 approx); German; Japanese (tr. details partial).
- **Midnight Robber**: French (*La Voleuse d'anges*, Denoël); Spanish.
- **The Salt Roads**: French (*Les Routes du sel*); German, Portuguese.
- Translation coverage is moderate; specific translator/year records for some editions are partial.

## notable recognition
- World Fantasy Award (*Sister Mine* nominee; "Greedy Choke Puppy" collection).
- John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer (1999).
- Locus Award, Sunburst Award (multiple).
- Norton Damon Knight Grand Master — SFWA Damon Knight Memorial Grand Master (2021, as first Black woman recipient).

## brief bio
Nalo Hopkinson was born in Kingston, Jamaica in 1960, raised in Guyana, Trinidad, and Canada, and now teaches at UC Riverside. Her fiction imports Caribbean language, cosmology, and historical memory into SF in ways that reshaped what SFF could sound like after the 1990s. She was the inaugural SFWA recipient of the Grand Master honour as the first Black woman; her *So Long Been Dreaming* anthology (2004) is a foundational curricular text for postcolonial SF. She writes and teaches alongside Saab Lofton, other emerging diasporic SFF voices.
