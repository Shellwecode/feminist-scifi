# N. K. Jemisin

## name
Nora Keita Jemisin

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
2002–present

## monumental works
- **The Fifth Season** (2015) / **The Obelisk Gate** (2016) / **The Stone Sky** (2017) — Broken Earth trilogy
    - form: novel trilogy
    - A dying Earth of periodic civilisation-ending seasons, ruled by the Sanzed Empire, controlled through the enslaved "orogene" class. First author ever to win the Hugo for Best Novel three consecutive years.
- **The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms** (2010)
    - form: novel
    - Inheritance-of-empire fantasy told by a provincial outsider; a debut that marked Jemisin as a major new voice.
- **The City We Became** (2020) / **The World We Make** (2022)
    - form: novel duology
    - New York's boroughs are personified as avatars defending the city from a Lovecraftian incursion.

## themes
race, power, ecology, colonialism, embodiment, gender

## influences
- Octavia Butler (direct, repeatedly; Jemisin dedicates *How Long 'Til Black Future Month?* in part to the Black SF tradition Butler made visible).
- Ursula K. Le Guin's worldbuilding rigour (Jemisin has cited in interviews).
- Toni Morrison and the Black literary tradition outside SF.

## influenced
- Has reshaped the Hugo electorate and the commercial face of genre SF; her three consecutive Best Novel Hugos (2016, 2017, 2018) broke the record.
- Rivers Solomon, P. Djèlí Clark, Tochi Onyebuchi, Premee Mohamed cite Jemisin in acknowledgements and interviews.

## translation history
- Broken Earth trilogy: French (*La Cinquième Saison* etc., tr. Michelle Charrier, Nouveaux Millénaires / J'ai Lu, 2017–19); German (*Die Zerbrochene Erde*, tr. Susanne Gerold, Knaur, 2017–19); Japanese (*第五の季節*, tr. 小野田和子 Onoda Kazuko, Sōgensha, 2020–21); Spanish (Nova, tr. Silvia Schettin Pérez); Russian, Italian, Polish, Chinese.
- Widely translated; translator attribution is generally well-documented in publisher listings.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award for Best Novel, three consecutive years (2016, 2017, 2018) — the only author ever to do so.
- Nebula Award (2018, *The Stone Sky*).
- Locus Awards (multiple).
- MacArthur Fellowship (2020) — second SF writer after Octavia Butler.

## brief bio
N. K. Jemisin grew up in New York and Mobile, Alabama, earned a master's in counselling psychology, and worked as a career counsellor while building a fiction career in the late 2000s. She went full-time after the Broken Earth trilogy broke Hugo records. Her essays (collected in *How Long 'Til Black Future Month?*, 2018) are sharp arguments about race, power, and the structure of genre. She lives in Brooklyn and in 2020 received the MacArthur Fellowship, explicitly framing herself as working in Butler's lineage.
