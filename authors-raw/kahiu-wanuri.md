# Wanuri Kahiu

## name
Wanuri Kahiu

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Kenya (African, continental)

## years active
2008–present

## monumental works
- **Pumzi** (2009)
    - form: short film (as writer/director)
    - 21-minute post-apocalyptic Nairobi where water is scarce and dreams are banned; a museum-worker glimpses a living tree outside. Widely cited as a foundational work of African SF cinema; Sundance premiere 2010.
- **Rafiki** (2018)
    - form: feature film (writer/director)
    - Not SF, but notable: first Kenyan film at Cannes; banned in Kenya for its lesbian content.
- **"Afrofuturism Is the New Black"** / "Afrobubblegum" (essays and TED talk, 2017)
    - form: essay / talk
    - Coined the concept of "Afrobubblegum" — fun, hopeful African storytelling.

## themes
ecology, post-apocalypse, gender, colonialism, utopia

## influences
- Octavia E. Butler — Kahiu has repeatedly cited Butler's *Parable of the Sower* in interviews and planned to adapt it into a film (announced 2019; status variable since).
- Kenyan ecological concerns (water scarcity and climate crisis shape *Pumzi* directly).

## influenced
- *Pumzi* is now taught in Afrofuturism courses globally; its visibility shaped the next decade of African genre filmmaking.
- Named in the *New York Times* and Sundance Institute discussions of African SF cinema.
- *Parable of the Sower* adaptation announcement (with producer Nicole Kassell) raised visibility of Butler's adaptation pipeline.

## translation history
- Her work is primarily filmic; *Pumzi* has circulated globally with subtitles in multiple languages. No fiction book-length translations specifically.
- Essays and talks translated for TED audiences.
- **Note**: the author is primarily a filmmaker; listing her stretches the category toward screenplay/essay/media. Included because her impact on African SF storytelling is canonical.

## notable recognition
- Cannes selection (*Rafiki*, 2018).
- Sundance premiere (*Pumzi*, 2010).
- Africa Movie Academy Awards, Silver Hugo (Chicago International Film Festival).

## brief bio
Wanuri Kahiu is a Nairobi-based filmmaker whose 2009 short film *Pumzi* is one of the defining texts of contemporary African SF, influential across written and cinematic traditions. She coined "Afrobubblegum" as a counter-movement to Western framings of African storytelling as tragic. Her feature *Rafiki* (2018) was the first Kenyan film to premiere at Cannes. She has worked on US TV productions and on the development of an Octavia Butler adaptation. She is included here as a proposed expansion of the dataset beyond strictly prose fiction — see research-log.md for notes on category ambiguity.
