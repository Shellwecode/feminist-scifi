# Kim Bo-Young

## name
Kim Bo-Young / 김보영

## primary writing language
Korean

## country / region
South Korea (East Asian)

## years active
2004–present

## monumental works
- **『당신을 기다리고 있어』 *Dangsineul Gidarigo Isseo* (*I'm Waiting for You*) (2015)**
    - form: novella / epistolary letters
    - A pair of lovers separated by relativistic time dilation try to meet on a returning spaceship. English tr. Sophie Bowman and Sung Ryu, Harper Voyager, 2021.
- **『7인의 집행관』 *Chirinui Jiphaenggwan* (*The Seven Executioners*) (2013)**
    - form: novel
    - Seven parallel lives judged one after another.
- **『우리가 다시 만날 세계』 *Uriga Dasi Mannal Segye* (*The World We Meet Again*)** (2022)
    - form: novel
    - Queer feminist parallel-worlds novel; widely discussed in Korean SF.
- **『얼마나 닮았는가』 *Eolmana Dalmannneunga* (*On the Origin of Species and Other Stories*, English ed. 2021)**
    - form: short-story collection (English compilation)
    - English edition translated by Sophie Bowman and Sung Ryu.

## themes
time, family/kinship, AI/machine-consciousness, power, embodiment

## influences
- Djuna (widely reported; Kim Bo-Young has acknowledged Djuna's importance to the formation of Korean SF — see *Readymade Bodhisattva* introduction).
- Ursula K. Le Guin — cited in interviews.

## influenced
- Her success in Korea, followed by English publication with Harper Voyager, paved the way for other Korean SF writers — Kim Choyeop among them.
- Worked as story consultant on Bong Joon-ho's *Snowpiercer* in its early phase (reported in Korean press and interviews; confirm attribution).

## translation history
- **I'm Waiting for You: And Other Stories**: English tr. **Sophie Bowman and Sung Ryu**, Harper Voyager (US) / HarperVoyager UK, 2021.
- **On the Origin of Species and Other Stories**: English tr. **Joungmin Lee Comfort and Sora Kim-Russell**, Kaya Press, 2021.
- French: selected stories in Decrescenzo's Korean-SF anthologies.
- Japanese: *あなたのための愛を、待っているわ* and others, translated by Kang Banghwa and others (Hayakawa).
- Chinese editions.

## notable recognition
- Korean SF Award (multiple).
- Her English-language debut was one of the first major Korean-SF translations of the 2020s wave.

## brief bio
Kim Bo-Young is widely considered one of Korea's foremost SF writers. She published her first story in 2004 and won the Korean SF Novel Contest with her first novel. Her work is structurally ambitious — frequently deploying relativistic physics, parallel worlds, and multi-life conceits — and she has spoken in interviews about refusing the distinction between "hard" and literary SF. She also consults on film. Two English volumes appeared in 2021 to strong reception, positioning her alongside Bora Chung and Kim Choyeop as leading figures in Korean SF's international breakthrough.
