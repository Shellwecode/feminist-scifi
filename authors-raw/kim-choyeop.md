# Kim Choyeop

## name
Kim Choyeop / 김초엽

## primary writing language
Korean

## country / region
South Korea (East Asian)

## years active
2017–present

## monumental works
- **『우리가 빛의 속도로 갈 수 없다면』 *Uriga Bicheui Sokdoro Gal Su Eopsdamyeon* (*If We Can't Go at the Speed of Light*) (2019)**
    - form: short-story collection
    - Seven stories of long-distance space travel, isolation, and care. Massive bestseller in Korea (300,000+ copies). English: *If We Can't Go at the Speed of Light*, tr. Anton Hur, HarperVoyager, 2024.
- **『방금 떠나온 세계』 *Banggeum Ttonaon Segye* (*The World We Just Left*) (2021)**
    - form: short-story collection
    - Disability and biotech-mediated embodiment.
- **『므레모사』 *Memoryossa* (*Mnemosyne*) (2021)**
    - form: novel
    - Dark-tourism to a mysterious contaminated town.

## themes
embodiment, biology, family/kinship, AI/machine-consciousness, time

## influences
- Ted Chiang (Kim Choyeop has repeatedly cited "Story of Your Life" and Chiang's short-fiction form as an influence in Korean press interviews).
- Ursula K. Le Guin — cited.
- Kim Bo-Young — cited as a Korean-SF precursor.

## influenced
- Central figure of the 2019–present Korean SF commercial wave; her debut collection's sales redefined what Korean SF could be in mainstream bookstores.
- Younger Korean SF writers cite her as permission-giving example.

## translation history
- **If We Can't Go at the Speed of Light**: English tr. **Anton Hur**, HarperVoyager, 2024.
- Japanese: 『わたしたちが光の速さで進めないなら』, tr. Kang Banghwa, 早川書房 (Hayakawa), 2020.
- Chinese (traditional and simplified): multiple editions.
- Vietnamese, Thai, Spanish, French editions (publishers include Points Deux, Rizzoli).
- Individual stories have appeared in *Clarkesworld* and elsewhere, translators include Anton Hur and Sophie Bowman.

## notable recognition
- Korean SF Novel Award (2017).
- Munhakdongne Young Writer Award.
- Today's Author Award (Korea).

## brief bio
Kim Choyeop studied biochemistry and has hearing loss; she has written and spoken publicly about disability politics and about the limits of "cure" narratives in SF. She debuted in 2017 and her 2019 collection *If We Can't Go at the Speed of Light* became one of the bestselling Korean SF works of the modern era, helping to establish a wide mainstream readership for the genre. Her fiction is quiet, relativistic, and care-centred. She lives in Seoul.
