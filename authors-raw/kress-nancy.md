# Nancy Kress

## name
Nancy Anne Kress (née Koningisor)

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
1976–present

## monumental works
- **"Beggars in Spain"** (1991, novella) / *Beggars in Spain* (1993, novel) / **Beggars and Choosers** (1994) / **Beggars Ride** (1996)
    - form: novella / novel trilogy
    - Genetically engineered "Sleepless" humans provoke social collapse; a landmark of 1990s biotech SF. Hugo and Nebula for the novella.
- **Probability Moon** / **Probability Sun** / **Probability Space** (2000–2002)
    - form: novel trilogy
    - First-contact / quantum-physics trilogy.
- **After the Fall, Before the Fall, During the Fall** (2012)
    - form: novella
    - Time-travel climate story; Nebula winner.
- **Yesterday's Kin** (2014) / Tomorrow's Kin (novel expansion 2017)
    - form: novella / novel
    - Aliens arrive to warn of a biological threat; intra-family drama meets xenobiology.

## themes
biology, power, labor, first-contact, dystopia

## influences
- Samuel R. Delany — Kress studied with Delany at SUNY Buffalo and has cited him in interviews.
- James Tiptree Jr. — cited in introductions and Kress's own essays.

## influenced
- Long-running Clarion / Clarion West instructor; direct teaching influence on many 1990s–2020s writers.
- Ted Chiang has cited Kress's "Beggars in Spain" novella as a formative short-form SF model (in interviews for *Stories of Your Life*).

## translation history
- *Beggars in Spain*: French (tr. Jean-Daniel Brèque, 1994, Denoël); German (*Bettler in Spanien*, tr. Biggy Winter, Heyne); Japanese (*ベガーズ・イン・スペイン*, tr. 金子司 Kaneko Tsukasa, Hayakawa); Italian, Spanish.
- Other Kress novels translated more sparingly; translator records partial.

## notable recognition
- Six Nebula Awards (as of 2024) — one of the most Nebula-decorated writers.
- Two Hugo Awards (Best Novella).
- John W. Campbell Memorial Award (2003, *Probability Space*).
- Locus Awards.

## brief bio
Nancy Kress taught elementary school and worked in advertising before becoming a full-time writer in the 1990s. "Beggars in Spain" (1991) — exploring a genetically engineered population that does not need sleep — crystallised her career-long interest in biotech-driven inequality. She has been a regular Clarion West and Taos Toolbox instructor, shaping a generation of short-form SF writers. She is also a longtime columnist for *Writer's Digest* and the author of several instructional books on fiction craft. She lives in Seattle.
