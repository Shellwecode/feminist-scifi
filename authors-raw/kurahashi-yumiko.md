# Kurahashi Yumiko

## name
Kurahashi Yumiko / 倉橋由美子

## primary writing language
Japanese

## country / region
Japan (East Asian)

## years active
1960–2005

## monumental works
- **『聖少女』 *Seishōjo* (*The Holy Virgin*; English title varies) (1965)**
    - form: novel
    - Taboo-breaking psychological novel with speculative elements.
- **『アマノン国往還記』 *Amanon-koku Ōkanki* (*A Round Trip to the Land of Amanon*) (1986)**
    - form: novel
    - A missionary returns to a far-future Japan-analog that has become a matriarchal state, "Amanon." An extended feminist SF/satire; won the Izumi Kyōka Prize. Not translated to English.
- **『パルタイ』 *Parutai* (*The Party*) (1960)**
    - form: short story
    - Her debut; ambiguous allegorical fiction about Japanese Communist Party activism.
- **『スミヤキストQの冒険』 *Sumiyakisuto Q no Bōken* (*The Adventures of Sumiyakist Q*) (1969)**
    - form: novel
    - Satirical-SF anti-utopia.

## themes
gender, dystopia, power, weird/horror, religion/myth

## influences
- Kafka, Sartre, Beauvoir (explicitly cited in Kurahashi's own essays; her MA thesis at Meiji University was on Sartre).
- Mishima Yukio (who publicly praised her early work).

## influenced
- Cited by later Japanese feminist writers including Tsushima Yūko and by SF critic Kotani Mari (*Techno-Gynesis*, 1994).
- English-language reception is limited but growing; *Cruel Fairy Tales for Adults* appeared in English translation.

## translation history
- **『パルタイ』 ("Partei")**: translated into English by Yukiko Tanaka and Elizabeth Hanson in *This Kind of Woman: Ten Stories by Japanese Women Writers, 1960–1976* (Stanford UP, 1982).
- **『アマノン国往還記』**: **NOT translated into English** — flag.
- **『夢の浮橋』 *The Bridge of Dreams***: translated into English as *The Floating Bridge of Dreams* (tr. details partial; Japan Foundation publication).
- **『大人のための残酷童話』 *Cruel Fairy Tales for Adults***: translated into English (Pushkin Press, forthcoming / limited editions — confirm).
- French: selected novels translated by Madeleine Lévy-Farnoux (Les Belles Lettres editions).
- German translations exist but records partial.

## notable recognition
- Izumi Kyōka Prize (1987, *A Round Trip to the Land of Amanon*).
- Tamura Toshiko Prize (1961).
- Joryū Bungakushō (Women's Literature Prize, 1963).

## brief bio
Kurahashi Yumiko emerged in 1960 with "Partei," a debut story that made her immediately one of the most discussed young writers in Japan. She held an MA in French literature from Meiji University; her literary allegiances were to European existentialism and the fantastic rather than to domestic realism. Her fiction combines erotic transgression, political allegory, and genre-speculative structures. *A Round Trip to the Land of Amanon* (1986) is her most sustained work of feminist SF but remains untranslated into English, a significant gap. She died in Tokyo in 2005.
