# Margo Lanagan

## name
Margo Lanagan

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Australia (Anglophone)

## years active
1990–present

## monumental works
- **Black Juice** (2004)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Ten dark, oblique stories; includes "Singing My Sister Down". World Fantasy Award.
- **Tender Morsels** (2008)
    - form: novel
    - Brutal reimagining of the "Snow-White and Rose-Red" tale across an otherworld refuge. Printz Honor; World Fantasy Award.
- **Sea Hearts** (2012, US title *The Brides of Rollrock Island*)
    - form: novel
    - Selkie-community novel on a remote island; meditative horror of gender economics.
- **"Singing My Sister Down"** (2004)
    - form: short story
    - Ritual tar-pit execution observed by the condemned girl's family; widely anthologised.

## themes
weird/horror, gender, family/kinship, embodiment, religion/myth

## influences
- Peter Carey and the Australian magical-realist tradition (acknowledged in interviews).
- Angela Carter — often compared and acknowledged as a touchstone.

## influenced

## notable recognition
- World Fantasy Award (2005, *Black Juice*; 2010, *Tender Morsels*; 2012 for "Sea Hearts" novella).
- Ditmar and Aurealis Awards (multiple, Australian national awards).
- Michael L. Printz Honor (2009, *Tender Morsels*).

## brief bio
Margo Lanagan, born in Waratah, NSW, worked as a technical editor while writing YA and adult short fiction. Her breakthrough collection *Black Juice* (2004) placed her in the forefront of 2000s weird-literary short fiction with an unusually precise, unsettled voice. *Tender Morsels* (2008) was controversial for its unflinching handling of sexual violence in YA form. She teaches creative writing and lives in Sydney. Her work straddles SF, fantasy, horror and weird, and she is the most internationally decorated Australian woman short-fiction writer of the 2000s–10s.
