# Ann Leckie

## name
Ann Leckie

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
2006–present

## monumental works
- **Ancillary Justice** (2013)
    - form: novel
    - The last remnant of a shattered starship AI — now inhabiting a single human body — pursues revenge through the multi-system Radch empire. First novel to win Hugo, Nebula, Arthur C. Clarke, BSFA, and Locus First-Novel Award.
- **Ancillary Sword** (2014) / **Ancillary Mercy** (2015)
    - form: novel sequels
    - Imperial Radch trilogy continues; moves from revenge-plot to sustained post-imperial politics.
- **Provenance** (2017)
    - form: novel
    - Standalone in the same universe; cultural-heritage heist.
- **Translation State** (2023)
    - form: novel
    - Legal-status SF at the edge of the Radchaai, Presger, and Geck treaty space.

## themes
AI/machine-consciousness, gender, power, colonialism, language, space-opera

## influences
- C.J. Cherryh (Leckie has repeatedly cited Cherryh, particularly *Cyteen* and the Alliance-Union books, in interviews and on her blog).
- Lois McMaster Bujold — acknowledged.
- Ursula K. Le Guin — acknowledged.

## influenced
- The "distributed AI narrator" and its use of the gender-neutral default pronoun "she" (which the Radchaai language does not mark for gender) has become a widely imitated device. Arkady Martine, Yoon Ha Lee, and others have spoken about the model Leckie set.

## translation history
- *Ancillary Justice*: French (*La Justice de l'ancillaire*, tr. Patrick Marcel, 2014, J'ai Lu); German (*Die Maschinen*, tr. Bernhard Kempen, Heyne, 2014); Japanese (*叛逆航路*, tr. 赤尾秀子 Akao Hideko, 2015, Tokyo Sogensha); Spanish (Ediciones B, tr. Silvia Schettin); Polish (Prószyński), Czech, Italian, Chinese, Russian.
- Sequels translated into most of the same languages.
- Translator records well-documented in Hugo/award publicity.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award for Best Novel (2014).
- Nebula Award for Best Novel (2014).
- Arthur C. Clarke Award (2014).
- BSFA Award (2014).
- Locus Award (Best First Novel, 2014).

## brief bio
Ann Leckie worked as a waitress, rodman, lunch lady, and recording engineer before selling her first story to *Strange Horizons* in 2006. *Ancillary Justice* (2013) was a debut of unusual impact, sweeping five major awards simultaneously and reshaping readers' expectations for genre-literary space opera. Her Radchaai universe's use of "she" as the unmarked pronoun helped mainstream discussions of grammatical gender in SF. She edits *GigaNotoSaurus* (2010–14) and lives in St. Louis.
