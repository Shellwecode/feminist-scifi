# Tanith Lee

## name
Tanith Lee

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United Kingdom (Anglophone)

## years active
1968–2015

## monumental works
- **The Birthgrave** (1975)
    - form: novel
    - Amnesiac warrior-woman rises from a volcano; feminist planetary romance that revitalised the sword-and-planet mode.
- **Don't Bite the Sun** (1976) / **Drinking Sapphire Wine** (1977)
    - form: novel duology
    - Decadent far-future city where shapeshifting, suicide, and reincarnation are consumer choices; YA-adjacent, genderqueer SF.
- **Biting the Sun** (1999, omnibus reissue)
- **The Silver Metal Lover** (1981)
    - form: novel
    - Young woman falls in love with an android performer; a key early text of synthetic-romance SF.
- **Tales from the Flat Earth** (1978–1987)
    - form: novel sequence
    - Lush cosmological fantasy; adjacent to but not SF.

## themes
gender, embodiment, AI/machine-consciousness, biology, dystopia, weird/horror

## influences
- Clark Ashton Smith, Jack Vance (self-reported in multiple interviews — the Dying Earth lineage is explicit in her work).
- Andre Norton (Lee said in several late interviews that Norton was her gateway author).

## influenced
- Storm Constantine has acknowledged Lee as a direct influence; their aesthetic overlap is substantial.
- Neil Gaiman has publicly credited Lee's *Tales from the Flat Earth* and *The Flat Earth* cycle as an influence on *The Sandman*.

## translation history
- *The Birthgrave*: French (*Le Feu de la déesse*, Pocket), German (*Die Göttin aus dem Feuer*, Heyne, tr. Lore Straßl), Japanese (*闇の公子*? — note: exact title-to-translation mapping for Lee's Japanese editions is partial), Polish.
- *The Silver Metal Lover*: French (*L'Amant métallique*, tr. Jean-Michel Pommier, Pocket), German; translator records partial.
- Lee's total corpus is massive (90+ novels); many are untranslated into any language, and English remains the sole edition for most of her late work.

## notable recognition
- British Fantasy Award (1980, "The Gorgon"; 1984, "Elle est trois, (La Mort)").
- World Fantasy Award (1983, "The Gorgon" — novelette).
- World Fantasy Life Achievement Award (2013).
- Lambda Literary Award (bisexual SF).

## brief bio
Tanith Lee sold her first story at 21 and her first novel, a children's book, at 24, before *The Birthgrave* (1975) made her name in the adult market. She published prolifically across SF, fantasy, horror, and children's fiction — more than 90 novels and 300 short stories — often to small or specialist presses after the big-house market cooled in the 1990s. Her work was consistently queer, sensuous, and formally experimental. She died of breast cancer in 2015. Much of her backlist has been reissued digitally through her estate.
