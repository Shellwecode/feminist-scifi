# Doris Lessing

## name
Doris May Lessing (née Tayler)

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United Kingdom / born Iran, raised in Southern Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe) (Anglophone)

## years active
1950–2013

## monumental works
- **Canopus in Argos: Archives** (1979–1983)
    - form: novel sequence (5 novels: *Shikasta*, *The Marriages Between Zones Three, Four, and Five*, *The Sirian Experiments*, *The Making of the Representative for Planet 8*, *The Sentimental Agents in the Volyen Empire*)
    - Cosmic-scale SF sequence in which galactic empires shape human history; mystical-Sufi inflected.
- **The Memoirs of a Survivor** (1974)
    - form: novel
    - Near-future collapse of a city, told by a woman watching from her window; visionary breaks in the wall of her apartment.
- **Briefing for a Descent into Hell** (1971)
    - form: novel
    - Inner-space SF: a man's psychiatric breakdown as cosmic voyage.
- **The Fifth Child** (1988)
    - form: novel (novella-length)
    - Atavistic "changeling" born into a middle-class English family; a genetic/weird-horror fable.

## themes
dystopia, religion/myth, weird/horror, power, post-apocalypse, gender

## influences
- Sufism, particularly through Idries Shah (Lessing wrote at length about this; see her essays in *A Small Personal Voice*).
- Olaf Stapledon — she cited *Last and First Men* and *Star Maker* as structural models for Canopus in Argos.

## influenced
- Her 1976 PEN-SFRA engagement with the SF genre, and her public defence of SF during her Nobel acceptance, lent literary legitimacy to genre SF in ways that benefited feminist-SF writers in particular.
- Margaret Atwood corresponded with and praised Lessing; Atwood's speculative fiction arguments owe something to Lessing's precedent.

## translation history
- *Canopus in Argos* sequence: French (*Shikasta*, tr. Marianne Véron, 1982, Seuil); German (Hoffmann und Campe, various translators); Japanese (*シカスタ*, 1988, tr. 山崎勉 Yamazaki Tsutomu, Fukutake Shoten); Spanish, Italian, Dutch, Swedish.
- *The Memoirs of a Survivor*: widely translated, including French (tr. Marianne Véron), German, Japanese, Spanish.
- Her Nobel status drove broad translation availability.

## notable recognition
- Nobel Prize in Literature (2007).
- James Tait Black Memorial Prize (1995).
- Prix Médicis étranger, Princess of Asturias Award, W.H. Smith Literary Award.
- Companion of Honour (declined Damehood in 1992).

## brief bio
Doris Lessing was born in Iran to British parents, raised on a farm in Southern Rhodesia, and moved to London in 1949. *The Grass Is Singing* (1950) and the Martha Quest cycle established her as a major postwar realist. Her turn to overt SF in the 1970s — *The Memoirs of a Survivor*, then the Canopus in Argos sequence — baffled mainstream critics but was embraced by the SF community. Her Nobel Prize citation specifically noted her "visionary power." She died in 2013 in London.
