# Nnedi Okorafor

## name
Nnedimma Nkemdili Okorafor

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Nigerian-American (African diaspora)

## years active
2001–present

## monumental works
- **Who Fears Death** (2010)
    - form: novel
    - In a post-apocalyptic future Sudan-analog, a "Ewu" (child of rape) sorceress confronts genocide. World Fantasy Award.
- **Binti** (2015) / **Binti: Home** (2017) / **Binti: The Night Masquerade** (2018)
    - form: novella trilogy
    - A Himba girl becomes the first of her people to attend Oomza University and mediates between humans and the Meduse species. Hugo and Nebula winner for the first novella.
- **Lagoon** (2014)
    - form: novel
    - Aliens arrive in the Atlantic off Lagos, Nigeria; multi-voice near-future SF.
- **Akata Witch / Sunny and the Mysteries of Osisi** (2011) and sequels
    - form: YA novel series
    - Nigerian/American teen in a magical society; "Nsibidi" as a working writing system.

## themes
race, gender, post-apocalypse, first-contact, religion/myth, colonialism, biology

## influences
- Octavia E. Butler — direct acknowledgement; Okorafor has written and spoken extensively about Butler as a foundational influence (see her *New York Times* piece, 2017).
- Tutuola, Amos (*The Palm-Wine Drinkard*) — cited as an early formative reading.
- Chinua Achebe (her dissertation advisor was in Nigerian literature).
- Ursula K. Le Guin — acknowledged.

## influenced
- Has coined the term "Africanfuturism" (distinct from Afrofuturism) in a 2019 blog essay that has since been widely adopted.
- A generation of younger African and African-diaspora SF writers cite her: Tochi Onyebuchi, Tomi Adeyemi, and others.
- Her HBO adaptation of *Who Fears Death* (in development since 2017, showrunner George R. R. Martin) has raised visibility.

## translation history
- **Who Fears Death**: French (*La Mort parfaite*, tr. Laurent Queyssi, Panini, 2019 approx); German (*Wer fürchtet den Tod*, tr. Claudia Kern); Spanish; Italian.
- **Binti**: French (*Binti*, tr. Laurent Queyssi, ActuSF, 2019); German (tr. Claudia Kern, Cross Cult); Spanish; Italian; Japanese (*ビンティ*, tr. 月岡小穂 Tsukioka Kosue, 2021, Hayakawa).
- **Lagoon**: French (tr. details partial); German (*Lagune*, Cross Cult).
- Widely translated; translator records documented per edition.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award, Nebula Award (*Binti*, 2016).
- World Fantasy Award (*Who Fears Death*, 2011).
- Locus Award; Wole Soyinka Prize for Literature in Africa (2008, *Zahrah the Windseeker*).
- Named a Library of Congress Living Legend.

## brief bio
Nnedi Okorafor was born in Cincinnati to Igbo Nigerian parents and has spent much of her life moving between the US and Nigeria. Partially paralysed in adolescence due to scoliosis surgery complications, she began writing during her recovery. Her fiction positions itself inside a specifically African cosmology — she distinguishes her "Africanfuturism" from Afrofuturism in that it centers African (not diasporic) futures. Her Butler lineage and her engagement with Igbo, Himba, and Sudanese material have made her one of the most widely read post-2010 SF writers globally.
