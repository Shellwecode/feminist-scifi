# Chinelo Onwualu

## name
Chinelo Onwualu

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Nigeria / Canada (African, continental / diaspora)

## years active
2012–present

## monumental works
- **Omenana Magazine** (founded 2014, co-founder and editor)
    - form: editorship
    - Co-founded with Chiagozie Fred Nwonwu; the first dedicated Africa-based online SFF magazine. Vastly expanded the platform for African SFF short fiction.
- **"What the Dead Man Said"** (2011) and other short stories in African and international genre venues
    - form: short story
    - Appeared in anthologies including *AfroSF* (ed. Ivor W. Hartmann, StoryTime, 2012 — landmark first pan-African SF anthology).
- **"The Time-Traveller's Tale" / various shorts** in *Strange Horizons*, *Omenana*, *Anathema*, *Clarkesworld*.

## themes
colonialism, religion/myth, gender, power, first-contact

## influences
- Nnedi Okorafor — acknowledged (the "Africanfuturism" framing Okorafor articulated in 2019 aligns with Onwualu's editorial position).
- Octavia E. Butler — acknowledged.
- African oral traditions (Igbo mythic cycles).

## influenced
- As editor of *Omenana*, Onwualu has published and shaped the careers of many African SFF writers of the 2010s–20s (Tade Thompson, Dare Segun Falowo, Wole Talabi, Suyi Davies Okungbowa, Eugen Bacon — though several of these are male or non-binary). The editorial-influence edge is central to her significance.
- Along with Chiagozie Fred Nwonwu and Geoff Ryman, instrumental in the 2010s infrastructure of African SFF publishing.

## translation history
- Onwualu's own short fiction has had limited translation into non-English languages; *Omenana*-published writers have been individually translated but the venue is English-language.
- **Flag**: an editorial figure of this importance for the field is typically less translated than her influence would suggest.

## notable recognition
- Nommo Award (African Speculative Fiction Society) multiple times, including as editor.
- Named to *BBC* and *Quartz Africa* lists of influential African cultural figures.
- Caine Prize shortlist.

## brief bio
Chinelo Onwualu is a Nigerian-Canadian writer and editor whose co-founding of *Omenana* in 2014 provided the first consistent African home for SFF short fiction, transforming infrastructure for the region. She worked as a journalist in Abuja before moving to Toronto, from which she edits, writes, and teaches. Her own fiction appears in African and international SFF venues; much of her significance is as a field-builder rather than a prolific novelist. She is represented here both for her fiction and for the editorial nexus that defined 2010s African SFF publishing.
