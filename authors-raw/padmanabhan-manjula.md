# Manjula Padmanabhan

## name
Manjula Padmanabhan

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
India (South Asian)

## years active
1970s–present

## monumental works
- **Harvest** (1997)
    - form: play (stage drama with strong SF premise)
    - A poor Bombay man sells his organs, one by one, to a wealthy American receiver via remote-monitoring technology; sustained speculative critique of organ markets and biopolitics. Onassis International Cultural Competition prize, 1997.
- **Escape** (2008)
    - form: novel
    - Post-apocalyptic India in which women have been eradicated; a solitary young woman, Meiji, is smuggled out of the all-male country by her "Generals" uncles.
- **The Island of Lost Girls** (2015)
    - form: novel
    - Sequel to *Escape*, continuing Meiji's journey.
- **Kleptomania: Ten Stories** (2004)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Spans realism and speculative modes.

## themes
biology, gender, power, dystopia, post-apocalypse, colonialism, labor

## influences
- Margaret Atwood's *The Handmaid's Tale* — Padmanabhan has cited it as a reference point in interviews about *Escape*.
- Indian political and feminist context (organ-donation scandals in 1990s India shaped *Harvest* directly).

## influenced
- *Harvest* is widely taught in Indian-English literature and postcolonial drama curricula.
- Has been cited by Bina Shah as an inspiration for *Before She Sleeps* (acknowledged in Shah's essays on feminist SF).
- The play's 2001 film adaptation *Deham* (dir. Govind Nihalani) extended its impact.

## translation history
- **Harvest**: translated into Hindi, German, French, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, Swedish — produced as stage work in many of these languages. Specific translator records partial; German translation by Salman Cineman / Staatstheater productions (verify).
- **Escape / The Island of Lost Girls**: English-only in most markets as of the mid-2020s; some French and German rights sold. Translation bibliography thinner than the play's.

## notable recognition
- Onassis International Cultural Competition for Theatre (1997, *Harvest*) — first Indian woman to win this prize.
- Padma Shri nominee-level visibility; Sahitya Akademi-adjacent recognition.

## brief bio
Manjula Padmanabhan is an Indian playwright, novelist, cartoonist, and illustrator based in Delhi and the US. Her comic strip *Suki* ran in *Pioneer* and other Indian newspapers for a decade. *Harvest* (1997) — a dystopian stage play about organ trade — remains one of the most internationally staged works of Indian English-language drama and is a central text of postcolonial SF. *Escape* (2008) explicitly takes on gender-based sex-selective abortion as dystopian premise, a direct speculative response to India's skewed sex ratio. She writes, illustrates, and comments across Indian media.
