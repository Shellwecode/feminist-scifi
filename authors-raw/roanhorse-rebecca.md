# Rebecca Roanhorse

## name
Rebecca Roanhorse

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States — Ohkay Owingeh Pueblo and African-American heritage (Indigenous Americas; included here as "other" — an under-represented category in the geographic scheme)

## years active
2017–present

## monumental works
- **"Welcome to Your Authentic Indian Experience™"** (2017)
    - form: short story
    - A Navajo man works as a "Vision Quest" guide for settler-tourists who virtually inhabit Indigenous bodies. Hugo, Nebula, and World Fantasy Awards.
- **Trail of Lightning** (2018) — first of the Sixth World series
    - form: novel
    - Post-climate-apocalypse Dinétah (Navajo Nation survives; the rest of the US has drowned); a monster-hunter confronts clan-specific powers. Locus Award winner.
- **Black Sun** (2020) — first of Between Earth and Sky
    - form: novel
    - Epic fantasy grounded in pre-Columbian Mesoamerican civilisations. Hugo, Nebula, Ignyte shortlists; Alex Award.

## themes
post-apocalypse, religion/myth, colonialism, race, gender, power

## influences
- N. K. Jemisin — cited (Roanhorse has talked about Jemisin's worldbuilding as permission-giving).
- Stephen Graham Jones (Blackfoot SFF writer) — contemporary acknowledged peer.
- Diné (Navajo) clan-law and Pueblo cosmological traditions — explicit in *Trail of Lightning*.
- Octavia Butler — acknowledged.

## influenced
- The Indigenous/SF conversation of the late 2010s–20s has been notably reshaped by her visibility; she co-edited the anthology *Never Whistle at Night: An Indigenous Dark Fiction Anthology* (2023, with Shane Hawk).
- The Astounding Award for Best New Writer went to her in 2018, the first Indigenous recipient.

## translation history
- **Trail of Lightning**: French (*Traqueuse de monstres*, tr. Jérôme Orsoni, Albin Michel Imaginaire, 2020); German (*Trail of Lightning*, Heyne, tr. Patricia Woitynek); Italian (Mondadori); Spanish, Polish, Russian, Turkish, Chinese.
- **Black Sun**: widely translated — French, German, Italian, Spanish, Polish, Czech, Portuguese.
- Translator attribution generally well-documented in Heyne, Albin Michel Imaginaire catalogs.

## notable recognition
- Astounding Award for Best New Writer (2018) — first Indigenous writer to win.
- Hugo Award (2018, "Welcome to Your Authentic Indian Experience™").
- Nebula Award (2017, same story).
- Locus Award (2019, *Trail of Lightning*).
- Alex Award (2021, *Black Sun*).

## brief bio
Rebecca Roanhorse is a citizen of the Ohkay Owingeh Pueblo and African-American on her mother's side, with Diné family ties through her husband. She practiced law before turning to SFF writing full-time in the mid-2010s. Her fiction consistently foregrounds Indigenous cosmologies as active SFF material — not as "inspiration" mined for genre but as living intellectual traditions whose sovereignty the work respects. Her "Welcome to Your Authentic Indian Experience™" (2017) became a flashpoint for discussions of the politics of Indigenous representation in SFF (see her essay "Postcards from the Apocalypse," 2018). She lives in Albuquerque, New Mexico.
