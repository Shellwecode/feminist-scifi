# Joanna Russ

## name
Joanna Russ

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
1959–2011

## monumental works
- **The Female Man** (1975, written 1971)
    - form: novel
    - Four versions of one woman across parallel timelines including the all-female Whileaway; a defining text of radical feminist SF.
- **We Who Are About To…** (1977)
    - form: novel
    - A survivor on a crashed colony refuses the reproductive imperative imposed on her by the male survivors; a pointed anti-Robinsonade.
- **The Adventures of Alyx** (stories 1967–70, collected 1976)
    - form: collection
    - Time-travelling proto-sword-and-sorcery heroine; early response to C. L. Moore.
- **"When It Changed"** (1972)
    - form: short story
    - Hugo-winning precursor to *The Female Man*: men arrive on all-female Whileaway.

## themes
gender, utopia, dystopia, power, language, labor

## influences
- C. L. Moore's Jirel (cited in Russ's own essays and the Alyx series).
- Vladimir Nabokov, whom she studied with at Cornell (documented in Samuel R. Delany's correspondence and Russ's essays).
- Samuel R. Delany — close colleague; their letters (*Alyx and After*) document mutual influence.

## influenced
- Nearly every feminist SF writer afterward: cited by Le Guin (who disagreed with her publicly and productively), Octavia Butler, Nicola Griffith, Nalo Hopkinson, Nisi Shawl.
- *How to Suppress Women's Writing* (1983) is a foundational text for feminist literary criticism and is widely cited outside SF.
- Donna Haraway cites Russ directly in "A Cyborg Manifesto" (1985).

## translation history
- **The Female Man**: French (*L'Autre moitié de l'homme*, tr. Henry-Luc Planchat, 1977); German (*Planet der Frauen*, tr. Claudia Franzen, 1979); Japanese (*フィーメール・マン*, 1983, tr. details partial); Italian, Spanish, Polish.
- **We Who Are About To…**: French and German editions; less widely translated.
- Essays/criticism translated more sporadically.

## notable recognition
- Hugo Award (1983, novella "Souls").
- Nebula Award (1972, "When It Changed").
- James Tiptree Jr. Award Retrospective (2002).
- Pilgrim Award for SF scholarship (1988).

## brief bio
Joanna Russ grew up in the Bronx, studied at Cornell (with Nabokov) and the Yale School of Drama, and taught at SUNY Binghamton and the University of Washington. Her fiction and criticism in the 1970s — *The Female Man*, "When It Changed," *How to Suppress Women's Writing* — made her the most confrontational theorist of gender within SF. She corresponded intensively with Samuel R. Delany and with James Tiptree Jr. (Alice Sheldon); those letters are a major record of the feminist-SF turn. Chronic illness curtailed her output from the late 1980s onward. She died in 2011.
