# Larissa Sansour

## name
Larissa Sansour

## primary writing language
English and Arabic (primary medium is film/visual art, not prose)

## country / region
Palestine / Denmark (MENA)

## years active
2003–present

## monumental works
- **A Space Exodus** (2009)
    - form: short film (5 min)
    - A Palestinian astronaut plants the Palestinian flag on the moon; a SF-inflected video-art response to Kubrick's *2001*.
- **Nation Estate** (2012)
    - form: short film (9 min) with accompanying photo series and short-story booklet
    - A future in which the Palestinian state is confined to a single vertical skyscraper, with each city on its own floor.
- **In the Future They Ate from the Finest Porcelain** (2015)
    - form: short film (29 min)
    - A "narrative-resistance" collective buries porcelain plates as fake archaeological evidence to claim a future territory; SF as politics of archaeology. Jameel Prize shortlist.
- **In Vitro** (2019)
    - form: two-channel film (28 min)
    - Underground orchard-bunker beneath Bethlehem after an ecological apocalypse; an elderly founder transmits memories to a clone successor. Danish pavilion at the 2019 Venice Biennale.

## themes
colonialism, post-apocalypse, religion/myth, ecology, time, embodiment, language

## influences
- Stanley Kubrick, Andrei Tarkovsky — visually and structurally, cited by Sansour in interviews.
- Ursula K. Le Guin, Octavia Butler — cited in interviews as influences on her sense of SF as political storytelling.
- Palestinian documentary-archive tradition (Emily Jacir, others).

## influenced
- *Nation Estate* and *In the Future They Ate from the Finest Porcelain* are widely cited in contemporary art criticism of Arab and Palestinian futurism.
- With Sophia Al-Maria, Monira Al Qadiri, Larissa Sansour forms a loose Arab-Futurist/Gulf-Futurist triad that has shaped 2010s–20s critical and curatorial frameworks.

## translation history
- Films circulate with multi-language subtitles; Arabic, English, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Danish subtitling.
- **In the Future They Ate from the Finest Porcelain** book-edition: Sternberg Press, 2016.
- **Nation Estate**: booklet-edition short story with film.
- **Note**: primarily filmic/video-art work. Category extension like Kahiu (Kenya) and Al-Maria (Qatar).

## notable recognition
- Danish Pavilion, 58th Venice Biennale (2019).
- Jameel Prize shortlist (2018).
- Jarman Award shortlist.
- Exhibitions at MoMA, Tate Modern, Mathaf, the Centre Pompidou.

## brief bio
Larissa Sansour was born in East Jerusalem in 1973 and is based in London. Her family background (her father a medical doctor and peace activist in Bethlehem) and her training at NYU Film and Goldsmiths have shaped a body of work that consistently uses SF imagery — space exploration, clones, apocalyptic bunkers — to argue about Palestinian displacement, return, and future. She represented Denmark at the 2019 Venice Biennale. She is included here as a filmic/visual-art MENA speculative author to mark the genuine range of Arab futurism; see the research log for notes on the cross-media category extension.
