# Samanta Schweblin

## name
Samanta Schweblin

## primary writing language
Spanish

## country / region
Argentina (Latin American); based in Berlin

## years active
2002–present

## monumental works
- **Distancia de rescate** (*Fever Dream*) (2014)
    - form: short novel
    - A dying woman and a strange child trace a poisoning in the pampas; ecological horror with speculative edges. English tr. Megan McDowell, Riverhead 2017. Shortlisted for the Man Booker International Prize 2017.
- **Kentukis** (*Little Eyes*) (2018)
    - form: novel
    - Plush-toy robots sold as surveillance companions; anonymous "keepers" and "dwellers" across the world are paired. Clear-cut near-future SF. English tr. Megan McDowell, Riverhead 2020. Booker International shortlist 2020.
- **Siete casas vacías** (*Seven Empty Houses*) (2015)
    - form: short-story collection
    - National Book Award for Translated Literature 2022, tr. Megan McDowell.
- **Pájaros en la boca** (*Mouthful of Birds*) (2009)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Early weird-fiction collection; multiple speculative stories.

## themes
weird/horror, ecology, biology, power, family/kinship, embodiment

## influences
- Shirley Jackson (cited by Schweblin in multiple interviews).
- Flannery O'Connor, Franz Kafka (cited).
- Argentine fantastic (Cortázar, Silvina Ocampo) — acknowledged.

## influenced
- With Bazterrica and Mariana Enríquez, central to the 2010s–20s Latin American weird-literary wave in English translation.
- *Little Eyes* specifically has been cited in surveillance-studies criticism and in later SF novels of ambient AI — though direct self-report from later writers is less documented.

## translation history
- **Fever Dream**: English tr. **Megan McDowell**, Riverhead Books, 2017.
- **Little Eyes**: English tr. **Megan McDowell**, Riverhead Books, 2020.
- **Mouthful of Birds**: English tr. Megan McDowell, Riverhead, 2019.
- **Seven Empty Houses**: English tr. Megan McDowell, Riverhead, 2022.
- German (Suhrkamp, tr. Marianne Gareis), French (Grasset), Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, Chinese, Japanese, Russian editions.
- McDowell is Schweblin's primary English translator across all works.

## notable recognition
- Man Booker International shortlist 2017 and 2020.
- National Book Award for Translated Literature 2022 (*Seven Empty Houses*).
- Shirley Jackson Award.
- Named to the Bogotá39 list of best Latin American writers under 40 (2017).

## brief bio
Samanta Schweblin was born in Buenos Aires in 1978 and has lived in Berlin since 2012. Her work is usually classed as literary rather than genre, but the SF-adjacent content of *Little Eyes* and *Fever Dream* places her inside contemporary speculative fiction. Megan McDowell's translations since 2017 have made her one of the most internationally visible Spanish-language writers of her generation. She is a three-time Booker International finalist.
