# Bina Shah

## name
Bina Shah

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
Pakistan (South Asian)

## years active
2001–present

## monumental works
- **Before She Sleeps** (2018)
    - form: novel
    - Near-future Green City (a fictional south-central Asian city) is ruled by a theocratic state after a gender-imbalance crisis from a virus that killed mostly women; surviving women are forced into polyandrous "Wife" arrangements.
- **The Monsoon War** (*The Monsoon War*) (2024)
    - form: novel
    - Sequel to *Before She Sleeps*, continuing the Green City political dystopia.
- **A Season for Martyrs** (2014)
    - form: novel
    - Historical-political novel of Pakistan, with elements of fantastic narrative; not strictly SF but representative of her earlier hybrid work.
- **Essays and short fiction** in *The New York Times*, *Al Jazeera*, *Dawn*
    - form: essays / journalism

## themes
gender, dystopia, power, religion/myth, biology, family/kinship

## influences
- Margaret Atwood — direct and explicit; Shah has repeatedly cited *The Handmaid's Tale* as a structural model for *Before She Sleeps* (see her *Literary Hub* 2018 essay, "Why I Wrote a Feminist Dystopia").
- Manjula Padmanabhan's *Harvest* and *Escape* — cited.
- Rokeya Sakhawat Hossain — Shah has referenced "Sultana's Dream" in discussions of a specifically South Asian feminist-SF lineage.

## influenced
- *Before She Sleeps* has been translated and widely discussed in international feminist-SF circles; Shah's essays have been influential in situating South Asian women's dystopian writing in global conversation.
- She has also been a regular commentator for international newspapers on women in Pakistan, which gives her fiction an unusually concrete political grounding.

## translation history
- **Before She Sleeps**: Italian (*Prima che dormano*, Neri Pozza, tr. details partial); German (tr. details partial); Spanish (tr. Carmen Francí, Siruela); Portuguese (Portugal ed.); Polish edition.
- **A Season for Martyrs**: Urdu edition (Pakistan), French.
- English-language hardcover published by Delphinium (US, 2018) and by Harper (India).

## notable recognition
- University of Southampton Creative Writing Fellowship.
- Finalist for the DSC Prize for South Asian Literature.
- Karachi Literature Festival Coca-Cola Book Prize (shortlist).
- Featured in *The New York Times* opinion pages as a columnist on Pakistan.

## brief bio
Bina Shah was born in Karachi in 1972, educated at Wellesley College and Harvard, and returned to Pakistan where she has lived and worked since. She writes novels, short fiction, and journalism — the last often for *The New York Times* and *Al Jazeera English*. *Before She Sleeps* (2018) is a deliberately situated feminist dystopia, drawing on Pakistani gender-imbalance data (honor killings, sex-selective practices) to imagine a regional dystopian future. She teaches creative writing and has been vocal in Pakistani and international press on women's rights. She is a board member of International PEN.
