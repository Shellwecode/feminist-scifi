# Vandana Singh

## name
Vandana Singh

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
India / United States (South Asian; US-resident)

## years active
2002–present

## monumental works
- **The Woman Who Thought She Was a Planet and Other Stories** (2008)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Delhi-based stories of weird, ecological, and cosmological unbelonging.
- **Ambiguity Machines and Other Stories** (2018)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Expanded international reputation; Locus Award finalist; shortlisted for the Philip K. Dick Award.
- **Distances** (2008)
    - form: novella
    - Tiptree Award-winner-adjacent (shortlisted) novella; mathematician-protagonist navigates the ocean-world Sagara.
- **Of Wind and Fire**: uncollected novella-cycle
    - form: novellas
    - Climate-fiction cycle exploring solidarities across ecological disaster.

## themes
ecology, first-contact, gender, colonialism, biology, time, language

## influences
- Ursula K. Le Guin — direct and extensive; Singh has written essays about Le Guin and acknowledges her as a formative influence (e.g., her *Strange Horizons* essay on Le Guin).
- Octavia E. Butler — acknowledged.
- Indian mathematics and physics traditions (Singh holds a PhD in theoretical particle physics; her scientific training informs the SF directly).
- Premchand and the Hindi-Urdu literary tradition — acknowledged in her essays.

## influenced
- Through her editorial work on South Asian SFF anthologies (including *Breaking the Bow: Speculative Fiction Inspired by the Ramayana*, with Anil Menon, 2012) and essay-writing ("Alternate Visions: Some Musings on Diversity in SF," among others), she has shaped how South Asian SF is read in Anglophone venues.
- Younger South Asian SF writers (Sumana Roy, Shveta Thakrar, Aruni Kashyap — though gender-mixed) cite her.

## translation history
- Individual stories translated into Hindi, Bengali, French, Spanish, Italian, Chinese for anthology appearances.
- Collection-level translations into non-English languages are limited; *Ambiguity Machines* has been translated into French (tr. Sylvie Denis / collective, exact editor-translator split partial).
- **Note**: Singh writes in English, so "translation" here is outward-facing, not "into English."

## notable recognition
- Parallax Award of the Carl Brandon Society (2009, for *Distances*).
- Locus Award shortlist (2019, *Ambiguity Machines*).
- Philip K. Dick Award shortlist.
- Widely anthologised (*The Best of the Best Science Fiction*, Dozois; multiple year's-best volumes).

## brief bio
Vandana Singh was born in New Delhi, earned a PhD in theoretical particle physics, and is a professor of physics at Framingham State University in Massachusetts. Her SF is quiet, precise, and politically situated, usually centring South Asian protagonists navigating ecological, cognitive, or cosmological strangeness. She has co-edited key South Asian SFF anthologies, and her essays on the climate crisis and on diversity in SF have been influential in genre criticism. She writes in English directly but draws explicitly on Indian scientific, mythological, and linguistic material.
