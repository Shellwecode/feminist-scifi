# Karin Tidbeck

## name
Karin Tidbeck

## primary writing language
Swedish and English (writes in both; self-translates)

## country / region
Sweden (European non-English, though also writes directly in English)

## years active
2002–present

## monumental works
- **Jagannath: Stories** (2012)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Twelve stories ranging across Swedish folklore, biotech weirdness, and bureaucratic dystopia. Cheeky, precise, uncategorisable. James Tiptree Jr. Award Honor List (2012); Crawford Award winner (2013).
- **Amatka** (*Amatka*) (Swedish 2012, English 2017)
    - form: novel
    - In a colony where reality is stabilised by constantly "labelling" every object, an information assistant discovers what happens when objects are left unnamed. Linguistic-ontological SF.
- **The Memory Theater** (2021)
    - form: novel
    - Time-displaced apprentices escape a garden-world where children are tortured for a court's entertainment.

## themes
language, weird/horror, power, embodiment, religion/myth, time

## influences
- Tove Jansson (cited as formative — the Moomin stories' weird undertones).
- Ursula K. Le Guin — acknowledged.
- Scandinavian folk-supernatural tradition (*vittror*, *trolls*).
- Edited and translated Swedish fairy-tale material as well.

## influenced
- Jagannath's international impact (Tiptree Honor, Crawford) placed Tidbeck's weird, precise short fiction as a model for 2010s genre-slippery short-story writing.
- Has been cited alongside Helen Oyeyemi and Sofia Samatar as a key voice of 2010s "contemporary weird" short fiction.

## translation history
- **Jagannath**: published in English first (Cheeky Frawg, 2012), with the stories self-translated from Swedish by Tidbeck herself. The Swedish edition *Vem är Arvid Pekon?* predates it.
- **Amatka**: Swedish original 2012; English self-translation by Tidbeck, Vintage (Knopf Doubleday) / Vintage Random, 2017.
- **The Memory Theater**: written in English directly; Swedish edition translated subsequently by Tidbeck herself.
- French (*Amatka*, tr. Luvan, La Volte, 2019); German, Spanish, Italian, Polish editions.

## notable recognition
- Crawford Award (2013, *Jagannath*).
- James Tiptree Jr. Award Honor List (2012).
- Locus Award for Best Collection (2013, *Jagannath* — nominee).
- Science Fiction & Fantasy Translation Award.

## brief bio
Karin Tidbeck lives in Malmö, Sweden. They are a professional live-action-roleplay (LARP) organiser as well as a writer and literary translator, and their fiction carries a studied respect for folklore, role-playing-game economies, and weird bureaucratic minutiae. Tidbeck is bilingual and self-translates between Swedish and English, which is unusual in SF publishing and is itself worth recording (see "translation history" above). They have used they/them pronouns in interviews since the late 2010s; included here with this noted for accuracy.
