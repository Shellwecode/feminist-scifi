# Olga Tokarczuk

## name
Olga Nawoja Tokarczuk

## primary writing language
Polish

## country / region
Poland (European non-English)

## years active
1989–present

## monumental works
- **Dom dzienny, dom nocny** (*House of Day, House of Night*) (1998)
    - form: novel (mosaic)
    - Multi-voiced novel of a Lower Silesian village, with dream-material, folk-speculative threads, and historical ghosts.
- **Bieguni** (*Flights*) (2007)
    - form: novel
    - Fragmented novel-essay on travel, anatomy, preservation of the body; contains several speculative set-pieces. Man Booker International Prize 2018.
- **Prowadź swój pług przez kości umarłych** (*Drive Your Plow Over the Bones of the Dead*) (2009)
    - form: novel
    - A retired engineer in rural Poland investigates deaths she attributes to animals taking revenge; William Blake, astrology, animal rights.
- **Księgi Jakubowe** (*The Books of Jacob*) (2014)
    - form: novel (900+ pages)
    - Historical novel of the 18th-century Jewish messianic figure Jacob Frank; includes mystical-speculative material.

## themes
religion/myth, ecology, time, power, gender, weird/horror

## influences
- C. G. Jung (Tokarczuk is a trained clinical psychologist, Jungian lineage).
- Bruno Schulz — often cited as a Polish literary antecedent.
- Polish Romantic tradition (Mickiewicz); medieval Jewish mysticism in *The Books of Jacob*.

## influenced
- Cited by Polish and international contemporary authors. Her "constellation novel" form — fragmentary, accreting — has been influential well beyond genre SF.
- The 2019 Nobel citation framed her as a writer of "the crossing of boundaries as a form of life."

## translation history
- **Flights**: English tr. **Jennifer Croft**, Fitzcarraldo Editions (UK) / Riverhead (US), 2017. Man Booker International 2018.
- **Drive Your Plow**: English tr. **Antonia Lloyd-Jones**, Fitzcarraldo / Riverhead, 2018.
- **The Books of Jacob**: English tr. Jennifer Croft, Fitzcarraldo / Riverhead, 2021. Man Booker International shortlist 2022.
- **House of Day, House of Night**: English tr. Antonia Lloyd-Jones, Granta Books, 2002.
- Translated into 40+ languages.

## notable recognition
- Nobel Prize in Literature (2018; awarded in 2019 alongside Peter Handke's delayed 2019 Nobel).
- Nike Award (Poland's most important literary prize; multiple).
- Man Booker International Prize (2018, *Flights*).
- Prix Laure-Bataillon (France); Brückepreis; Austrian State Prize.

## brief bio
Olga Tokarczuk studied psychology at the University of Warsaw and practiced as a therapist before turning to literature full-time. She lives in Krajanów, a small village in Lower Silesia near the Czech border. Her work frequently crosses into speculative, mythic, and weird territory without adopting SF genre conventions — which is why this dataset includes her as a European speculative author whose affinities with SF (particularly around human/non-human boundaries and embodiment) are substantial. Her 2018 Nobel Prize recognised "a narrative imagination that with encyclopedic passion represents the crossing of boundaries as a form of life."
