# Tatiana Tolstaya

## name
Tatiana Nikitichna Tolstaya / Татьяна Никитична Толстая

## primary writing language
Russian

## country / region
Russia (European non-English)

## years active
1983–present

## monumental works
- **Кысь** *Kys'* (*The Slynx*) (2000)
    - form: novel
    - Two hundred years after "the Blast," Moscow has regressed to a pre-industrial village of mutated humans ruled by a despotic Murza; the protagonist, a scribe, slowly learns to read. A post-apocalyptic dystopia in a deliberately archaic Russian register.
- **На золотом крыльце сидели** *Na zolotom kryl'tse sideli* (*On the Golden Porch*) (1987)
    - form: short-story collection
    - Her debut collection, mixing realism with fantastical elements.
- **Ночь** *Noch'* (*Night*) (1988) and subsequent story collections
    - form: short-story collections
    - Short fiction in a dense, literary register.

## themes
post-apocalypse, language, power, religion/myth, dystopia

## influences
- Nikolai Gogol, Andrei Bely — cited in interviews.
- Her family's literary inheritance (her grandfather Alexei N. Tolstoy was a prominent Soviet SF author; her great-great-uncle was Leo Tolstoy).

## influenced
- *The Slynx* is widely taught in Slavic-studies courses as the key post-Soviet dystopia; Viktor Pelevin and Vladimir Sorokin are frequent comparative references but her influence is distinct.
- Western post-2000 writers of "literary post-apocalyptic" fiction sometimes cite *The Slynx* (e.g., Jesse Ball).

## translation history
- **The Slynx**: English tr. **Jamey Gambrell**, Houghton Mifflin / New York Review Books, 2003.
- **White Walls** (selected stories): English tr. Jamey Gambrell and others, NYRB Classics, 2007.
- **On the Golden Porch**: English tr. Antonina W. Bouis, Knopf, 1989.
- **Sleepwalker in a Fog**: English tr. Jamey Gambrell, Knopf, 1992.
- French (*Kys*, tr. Christine Zeytounian-Beloüs, Laffont); German (*Kys*, tr. Christiane Körner, Rowohlt); Italian, Spanish, Dutch, Polish, Swedish, Japanese, Chinese editions.

## notable recognition
- Triumph Prize (2001, *Kys'*).
- Bunin Prize (2013).
- Multiple Russian literary prizes.
- International recognition through Gambrell's translations and NYRB imprint.

## brief bio
Tatiana Tolstaya was born in Leningrad in 1951 into one of Russia's most distinguished literary families. She worked as an editor at Nauka publishing house before her 1983 debut with "On the Golden Porch." She taught at Princeton, Skidmore, and the University of Richmond during the 1990s, and has been a prominent Russian television co-host (*Shkola zlosloviya*, "School of Gossip," 2002–2014). *The Slynx* (2000) is her major SF contribution: a dense, satirical, linguistically inventive post-apocalyptic novel that took her fourteen years to finish. She lives in Moscow.
