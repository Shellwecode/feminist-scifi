# Élisabeth Vonarburg

## name
Élisabeth Vonarburg

## primary writing language
French

## country / region
France / Québec, Canada (European non-English; Francophone Canadian)

## years active
1977–present

## monumental works
- **Le Silence de la cité** (*The Silent City*) (1981)
    - form: novel
    - Post-apocalyptic future in which a near-immortal woman, raised in an underground city, is sent to an irradiated surface where mutant humans have developed matriarchal societies. Foundational work of Francophone feminist SF.
- **Chroniques du Pays des Mères** (*In the Mothers' Land*) (1992)
    - form: novel
    - Sequel-in-universe to *Le Silence de la cité*; imagines a fully matriarchal post-apocalyptic society in political depth. Grand Prix de l'Imaginaire.
- **Tyranaël** quintet (1996–1997)
    - form: novel sequence (5 volumes)
    - Multi-generational colonisation of an alien world; ecological and telepathic themes.
- **Reine de Mémoire** pentalogy (2005–2007)
    - form: novel sequence

## themes
gender, post-apocalypse, ecology, biology, first-contact, time

## influences
- Ursula K. Le Guin — direct acknowledgement; Vonarburg has translated Le Guin into French and cites her extensively in essays and interviews.
- Joanna Russ — acknowledged.
- Robert Silverberg — cited (Vonarburg's PhD work included Silverberg).

## influenced
- As longtime editor of *Solaris* magazine (Québec's primary SF magazine, from the late 1970s), Vonarburg shaped a generation of Francophone Canadian SF.
- The Prix Boréal (Québécois SF prize) named a major category in her honour.
- Aliette de Bodard and Francophone feminist SF writers of later generations cite her.

## translation history
- **The Silent City**: English tr. **Jane Brierley**, Porcepic Books, 1988.
- **In the Mothers' Land** (*Chroniques du Pays des Mères*): English tr. Jane Brierley, Bantam Spectra, 1992.
- **Reluctant Voyagers** (*Les Voyageurs malgré eux*): English tr. Jane Brierley, Bantam Spectra, 1995.
- Translated into Spanish, Italian, German, Romanian editions of various novels.
- Brierley was Vonarburg's primary English translator for over a decade.

## notable recognition
- Grand Prix de l'Imaginaire (multiple, 1982 for *Le Silence de la cité*, 1993 for *In the Mothers' Land*, others).
- Prix Boréal (multiple).
- Philip K. Dick Award special citation (1992, *In the Mothers' Land*).
- Aurora Award (Canadian Anglophone SF) for French and English work.

## brief bio
Élisabeth Vonarburg was born in Paris in 1947 and moved to Québec in 1973. She holds a doctorate in creative writing from the Université de Laval and was the literary director of *Solaris* magazine for nearly two decades. Her work is a direct Francophone extension of the Le Guin / Russ lineage of feminist SF and is widely taught in French-language SF studies. She has also translated SF into French (Le Guin, others). She lives in Chicoutimi, Québec.
