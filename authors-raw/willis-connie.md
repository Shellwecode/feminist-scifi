# Connie Willis

## name
Constance Elaine Trimmer Willis

## primary writing language
English

## country / region
United States (Anglophone)

## years active
1971–present

## monumental works
- **Doomsday Book** (1992)
    - form: novel
    - Oxford time-travel historian sent to 14th-century England lands in the Black Death year. Hugo and Nebula winner.
- **To Say Nothing of the Dog** (1998)
    - form: novel
    - Victorian time-travel comic novel; Hugo winner.
- **Blackout / All Clear** (2010)
    - form: novel diptych
    - Oxford historians trapped in the London Blitz. Joint Hugo and Nebula winner.
- **"Fire Watch"** (1982)
    - form: novella
    - First Oxford time-travel story; establishes the sequence. Hugo winner.

## themes
time, war, religion/myth, labor, dystopia

## influences
- Robert A. Heinlein (Willis has repeatedly cited *Have Space Suit — Will Travel* as a formative text in interviews and at Worldcons).
- Dorothy L. Sayers, Jerome K. Jerome, P.G. Wodehouse — the English comic tradition, openly cited for *To Say Nothing of the Dog*.
- Ward Moore's *Bring the Jubilee* — cited as a time-travel model.

## influenced
- Her hybrid of rigorous historical research with SF time-travel has been cited by later writers — Kage Baker's Company series, Kate Mascarenhas, Connie Willis herself remains a touchstone for character-warm time-travel SF.

## translation history
- *Doomsday Book*: French (*Le Grand Livre*, tr. Jean-Pierre Pugi, 1994, J'ai Lu); German (*Die Jahre des schwarzen Todes*, Heyne); Japanese (*ドゥームズデイ・ブック*, tr. 大森望 Ōmori Nozomi, 2003, Hayakawa); Spanish, Polish, Russian, Italian.
- *To Say Nothing of the Dog*: French (*Sans parler du chien*); German; Japanese (tr. Ōmori Nozomi).
- Blackout/All Clear: French, German, Japanese, various — some translator records partial.

## notable recognition
- 11 Hugo Awards, 7 Nebula Awards — more combined Hugos and Nebulas than any other writer as of 2024.
- SFWA Grand Master (2011).
- Damon Knight Memorial Grand Master; Science Fiction Hall of Fame.

## brief bio
Connie Willis grew up in Colorado, taught middle school English, and began selling short fiction in 1971. Her Oxford time-travel stories — begun with "Fire Watch" in 1982 — constitute the core of her reputation. Willis's voice moves easily between broad farce (*To Say Nothing of the Dog*) and harrowing historical disaster (*Doomsday Book*, *Blackout/All Clear*). She is among the most-awarded figures in the genre and a much-loved fixture at Worldcon. She lives in Greeley, Colorado.
