# Xia Jia

## name
Xia Jia / 夏笳 (pen name of Wang Yao / 王瑶)

## primary writing language
Chinese (Mandarin)

## country / region
China (East Asian)

## years active
2004–present

## monumental works
- **「百鬼夜行街」 ("A Hundred Ghosts Parade Tonight") (2010)**
    - form: short story
    - A boy raised by ghost-automata in a traditional Chinese ghost town; "science fantasy" style. English tr. Ken Liu, *Clarkesworld*, 2012.
- **「童童的夏天」 ("Tongtong's Summer") (2014)**
    - form: short story
    - Elderly grandfather is cared for by a telepresence robot controlled by a young woman; intergenerational near-future. English tr. Ken Liu.
- **「熱島」 ("Goodnight, Melancholy") (2016)**
    - form: novelette
    - Alan-Turing-adjacent narrative; historical SF.
- **『中国百科全书系列』 *Zhōngguó Bǎikē Quánshū* series** (2010s–20s)
    - form: linked short-story cycle
    - "Chinese Encyclopedia" series imagining near-future China; ongoing.

## themes
AI/machine-consciousness, family/kinship, religion/myth, time, ecology

## influences
- Chinese classical literary tradition (supernatural fiction: *zhiguai*, *chuanqi*); the "ghost town" mode is self-reported.
- Ursula K. Le Guin — Xia Jia's academic work at Peking University was on Le Guin; her PhD thesis was on contemporary Chinese SF.

## influenced
- As a critic and academic (now associate professor at Xi'an Jiaotong University), Xia Jia has shaped the critical reception of 2010s Chinese SF; she coined the phrase "science fiction realism" for a strand of Chinese SF.
- Her essay "What Makes Chinese Science Fiction Chinese?" (trans. Ken Liu, *Tor.com*, 2014) is a widely cited reference.

## translation history
- "A Hundred Ghosts Parade Tonight": English tr. **Ken Liu**, *Clarkesworld* #64, January 2012.
- "Tongtong's Summer": English tr. **Ken Liu**, in *Upgraded* (Wyrm, 2014).
- "Goodnight, Melancholy": English tr. **Ken Liu**, *Clarkesworld* #115, April 2016.
- Many stories collected in English in *Invisible Planets* (2016) and *Broken Stars* (2019), both edited and translated by Ken Liu, Tor.
- French: *Broken Stars* and other anthologies translated by Gwennaël Gaffric (Actes Sud).
- Italian, Spanish, German editions of anthologies.

## notable recognition
- Yinhe (Galaxy) Awards, multiple.
- Xingyun (Nebula) Awards, multiple.
- Academic: Ph.D. from Peking University in Chinese SF.

## brief bio
Xia Jia (Wang Yao) was born in 1984, studied at Peking University — first atmospheric science, then film studies, then Chinese literature — and earned her PhD with a dissertation on contemporary Chinese SF. She was one of the first Chinese women writers to break out internationally through Ken Liu's translations in *Clarkesworld* and subsequently through the *Invisible Planets* and *Broken Stars* anthologies. She is now an associate professor at Xi'an Jiaotong University and writes in a hybrid "science fiction realism" register that often folds folklore and traditional material into near-future settings.
