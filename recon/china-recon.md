# Reconnaissance: Chinese-Language Women's SF

A source-language survey of the field's shape — infrastructure, candidates, structural absences, sourcing reliability — conducted to inform Phase 2 expansion beyond the four authors already in the dataset (Hao Jingfang, Xia Jia, Chi Hui, Tang Fei). The memo follows the source priority rule: Chinese-language sources first, English secondary only as confirmation. Names given in original script (简体 for PRC, 繁體 for Taiwan/HK) with pinyin/Wade-Giles in parentheses on first mention.

## 1. Field infrastructure by region

### 1.1 Mainland (PRC)

**The dominant institution is still 《科幻世界》(*Science Fiction World*, Chengdu, est. 1979 as 《科学文艺》*Kēxué Wényì*).** This single magazine has functioned as the gatekeeper of "serious" SF since the 1980s reform-era revival. It administers the 银河奖 (Galaxy Award, since 1986) and effectively defines who is published, anthologised and read as SF in mainland publishing. Critically for a women's-SF mapping: every mainland women's-SF career we can verify in the dataset passes through 《科幻世界》 at some point (debut, prize, or editorship).

Key editorial figures, women in SF infrastructure:
- **杨潇 (Yáng Xiāo)** — vice-editor-in-chief of 《科学文艺》in the 1980s, organised the first 银河奖 (1986) and secured the 1991 World SF Convention bid for Chengdu. The most senior woman editor in the magazine's history. Cited in 张静's "灰姑娘" essay (科普创作评论 2019) as the figure who "kept the magazine alive" through the 1980s SF crackdown.
- **杨枫 (Yáng Fēng)** — joined 《科幻世界》2003; rose to vice-editor; left 2016 to found **八光分文化 (Bāguāngfēn / Eight Light Minutes)**, an independent SF press in Chengdu that has become the single most important non-magazine publisher of women's-authored SF. 八光分 published 顾适's collection 《莫比乌斯时空》 (2020), runs 《银河边缘》 (Galaxy's Edge Chinese edition, co-edited with Mike Resnick before his death), and co-founded the **冷湖科幻文学奖 (Lěnghú SF Award, 2018+)** — explicitly a competitor/complement to the older Galaxy Award.
- **程婧波 (Chéng Jìngbō)** — writer who also serves as editor: edited 《她：中国女性科幻作家经典作品集》(*She: Classic Works of Chinese Women SF Writers*, 33-author anthology, 2023), the first source-language canon-formation volume specifically for Chinese women's SF. Was one of the founding organisers of 华语科幻星云奖 and directed its first three ceremonies.

  > "收录了老中青三代具有代表性的33位对中国科幻进程有影响力的女性中国科幻作家，首次整体展现了这一群体的风采。"
  >
  > [translated from Simplified Chinese: original / English: "Includes 33 representative women SF writers across three generations — senior, middle, and emerging — who have had influence on the trajectory of Chinese SF; the first integral presentation of this group's range."]
  > — 中国出版传媒商报, https://www.cbbr.com.cn/contents/533/77124.html. *Secondary (publisher-trade press).* Confirms both the 33-author count and the "first" canon-formation claim.
- **姬少亭 (Jī Shàotíng)** — ex-Xinhua reporter, co-founder of Guokr, founded **未来事务管理局 (FAA / Future Affairs Administration, 2016+)** which runs the **「不存在科幻」WeChat / Douban / web platform**. FAA is now the dominant short-fiction venue outside 《科幻世界》's print pipeline; it runs an annual 春晚 (SF Spring Gala), and the 2026 Gala is reported to feature an all-women writer lineup ("Running Me" theme) for the first time. Independently verifiable; suggests editorial recognition of a critical mass that wasn't there a decade ago.

Award circuits:
- **银河奖** (1986–): magazine prize, conservative-canonical. First identifiable woman recipient in the published roster: **晶静 / 张静 (Jīng Jìng / Zhāng Jìng)** for 《神秘的声波》 (1986, 二等奖 / second prize, per her own retrospective). 《最美的眼睛》 (1985, 科学文艺) — likely the earliest published women's-SF short to receive prize recognition in the PRC.

  > "1986年1月，我的科幻小说处女作《神秘的声波》在《智慧树》发表，并获得全国首届科幻小说银河奖二等奖。为此，我应邀参加1986年5月15日在四川成都举办的首届银河奖颁奖大会。"
  >
  > [translated from Simplified Chinese: original / English: "In January 1986, my debut SF story *The Mysterious Sound-Wave* was published in *Tree of Wisdom*, and won second prize at the inaugural national Galaxy Award for SF. I was invited to attend the inaugural Galaxy Award ceremony held on 15 May 1986 in Chengdu, Sichuan."]
  > — 张静, 《"灰姑娘"华丽转身前后》, 科普创作评论 2019. *Primary source.* Note: the essay does not itself frame her as "first woman" — that inference is the recon's, based on the published winners roster. She continued through the late 80s with mythological-SF series (《女娲恋》《织女恋》《夸父追日》《盘古》) which reframe Chinese myth as SF — distinct from later cohorts.
- **华语科幻星云奖 / Xingyun / Chinese Nebula** (2010–): industry/professional prize, broader Sinophone scope but mainland-dominated. Multiple women winners across categories — 顾适 (2016 中篇金奖, 《嵌合体》), 程婧波 (multiple short and 中篇 gold), 迟卉 (2022 短篇金奖 《不做梦的群星》), 段子期 (2018–2020 新星奖金奖). Note: 《嵌合体》 was longlisted for the 2017 Hugo Best Novella, indicating the prize's translation-pipeline function.
- **冷湖科幻文学奖** (2018–): newer, runs through 八光分; tends to surface less-canonised voices.
- **晨星奖**, **引力奖** (China SF Readers' Choice): reader-vote awards; 昼温 won 引力奖 best short for both 《沉默的音节》(2018) and 《猫群算法》(2021).

Academic / critical infrastructure:
- **吴岩 (Wú Yán)**, professor at Southern University of Science and Technology, established the first PRC undergraduate SF course (1991), MA programme (2003), PhD direction (2015). PI of the National Social Science Foundation 《20世纪中国科幻小说史》 (*History of 20th-c. Chinese SF*, 2022, 豆瓣 35706867). His 《20世纪中国科幻小说史》 is the canonical secondary work in Chinese — important to verify what it says about women writers in Phase 2 (PDF was paywalled / corrupted via web fetch this session; Douban suggests it is structured chronologically and may underrepresent the 1980s women cohort beyond 张静).
- **宋明炜 (Sòng Míngwěi / Mingwei Song)**, Wellesley East Asian Studies — bilingual critic, the most active feminist-inflected scholarly voice. Published 《科幻的性别问题——超越二项性的诗学想象力》 ("The Gender Question of SF: Poetic Imagination Beyond Binarity") in 《上海文学》 2022 issue 5. His monograph 《看的恐惧：中国科幻诗学》 (*The Fear of Seeing: A Poetics of Chinese SF*) explicitly reads cyborg/posthuman/non-binary gender as a structural concern. He is the closest thing to a Sinophone Donna Haraway-ish reader; useful as a secondary-source anchor.
- **王德威 (Wáng Déwēi / David Der-wei Wang)**, Harvard — diasporic but Chinese-language. Has frequently anchored the academic argument that contemporary Chinese SF is a "new wave" worth taking seriously; less specifically gendered in focus.
- **石静远 (Shí Jìngyuǎn / Jing Tsu)**, Yale — invoked alongside Song and Wang in the Huxiu round-up of gender-in-Chinese-SF discourse.
- **陈楸帆 (Chén Qiūfān / Chen Qiufan)** — SF writer; author of the 2018 《光明日报》 essay 《科幻中的女性主义书写》 ("Feminist Writing in SF"), one of the few mainland-published critical treatments of the subject. He praises 赵海虹, 凌晨, 郝景芳, 夏笳, 迟卉 for their female-perspective work but argues mainland women SF authors tend toward "去性/性别化" (de-sexualisation / de-gendering) compared to male authors — a contestable but widely cited claim that should be tracked, not endorsed, in the dataset. *(Prior recon attributed this essay to 韩松 — corrected here from direct fetch.)*

  > "尽管在女性代表作家如赵海虹（《伊俄卡斯达》）、凌晨（《潜入贵阳》）、郝景芳（《流浪玛厄斯》）、夏笳（《中国科幻百科》）、迟卉（《归者无路》）中都通过女性角色视角完成了对想象性世界的探索与秩序重构与和解，但并未能借助科幻的认知框架更进一步的颠覆与讨论性别议题本身……造成很多时候中国女性科幻作品的"去性/性别化"倾向。"
  >
  > [translated from Simplified Chinese: original / English: "Although in representative women writers — 赵海虹 (*Iocasta*), 凌晨 (*Infiltrating Guiyang*), 郝景芳 (*Wandering Maers*), 夏笳 (*Encyclopedia of Chinese SF*), 迟卉 (*The Returner Has No Road*) — exploration of imagined worlds and order-reconstruction-and-reconciliation has been completed through female-character perspective, they have not been able to use SF's cognitive framework to further subvert and discuss gender issues themselves … producing, much of the time, a 'de-sexualisation / de-gendering' tendency in Chinese women's SF works."]
  > — 陈楸帆, 《科幻中的女性主义书写》, 光明日报 2018-09-26. *Secondary (critic-author).* The "去性/性别化" frame is Chen Qiufan's polemical claim, not a settled finding.
- **郁旭映** — appears as participant in 澎湃新闻's "科幻的边缘，边缘的科幻" roundtable with 王侃瑜, 马辰, 吕广钊. Younger critic; check for further publications in Phase 2.

Translation pipelines *into* Chinese (influence-context, not edges):
- Le Guin, Tiptree, Atwood, Butler all available in mainland translation by mid-2010s. Le Guin's 《黑暗的左手》 has had multiple PRC editions. This shapes the reading environment within which 夏笳, 郝景芳 came up — but documenting specific influence claims requires interview-by-interview verification.

### 1.2 Taiwan

A genuinely separate tradition — institutionally, generically, and politically. Taiwan SF has been more academically recognised earlier (交通大學 SF Research Centre, est. 2001, 葉李華), more queer-coded in the 1990s, and never institutionally tied to 《科幻世界》.

The originary text of Sinophone women's SF is **張曉風 (Zhāng Xiǎofēng)**'s 《潘渡娜》 (*Pandora*, 中國時報, 12 September 1968)** — credited as the first SF story published in Taiwan.

> *excerpt pending — PanSci article (https://pansci.asia/archives/144074) fetched this session did not contain the 1968-09-12 《潘渡娜》 sentence; only post-1980s Taiwan-SF coverage. Verification needs the 鄭政恆 明報月刊 piece or NCL Taiwan 中國時報 archive.* The story's bioethical-Pandora frame predates by years the male-dominated 1970s PRC second wave. This single fact substantively complicates `gaps.md`'s framing: the Sinophone SF chronology, when read in source language, has a woman at the foundational point. 張曉風 is primarily known as an essayist and is rarely categorised *as* an SF writer in English secondary literature, which is why the dataset misses her.

Taiwan award circuits:
- **皇冠大眾小說獎** (Crown Popular Novel Award, 1994+) — broad commercial-literary prize; SF writers won publication slots through it. 文旦's 《二四俱樂部》 (2000) and 張草's 《北京滅亡》 (2000, co-winner that year) are the SF-relevant cases. 文旦 is reported as a woman writer in the PanSci article; verify in Phase 2.
- **倪匡科幻獎** (Ni Kuang SF Award, 2001–2010) — ten editions, run through 交大. Most prominent winners (李伍薰, 柚臻) are male; women laureates appear thinner in the surfaced record. Worth a specific archival pass against the 交大科幻研究中心 site for women shortlistees.
- **臺灣文學獎金典獎** — the 2024 Grand Prize went to **平路 (Píng Lù)** for 《夢魂之地》 (*Land of Dream Souls*). Ping Lu is a long-established speculative-literary novelist; her 《行道天涯》 and 《何日君再來》 used historical-counterfactual modes. Cross-genre rather than core SF, but fits the dataset's existing tolerance for speculative-literary work (e.g. Wolf, Tokarczuk).

The clearest Taiwan candidate the dataset is missing is **洪凌 (Hóng Líng / Lucifer Hung, b. 1971)**. Trained at Sussex (MA, "Sexual Dissidence and Cultural Change") and Chinese University of Hong Kong (PhD, Cultural Studies); now associate professor in Gender Studies at 世新大學 (Shih Hsin). Active publisher since late 1980s. **Won the 1994 全球華人科幻小說獎 (Global Chinese SF Novel Award) Honorable Mention / 優選** before either 张静 or 凌晨 had been widely recognised across the Strait.

  > "1994年獲全球華人科幻小說獎優選，次年出版第一本中短篇小說集《肢解異獸》。"
  >
  > "洪凌的創作與理論研究聚焦於旁若文學、賽博龐克、後人類、後冷戰政治、罔兩等主題。"
  >
  > [translated from Traditional Chinese: original / English: "In 1994 received the Honorable Mention (優選) at the Global Chinese SF Novel Award; the following year published her first short-fiction collection *Dismembering Exotic Beasts*." / "Hong Ling's creative and theoretical work focuses on queer literature, cyberpunk, posthumanism, post-Cold War politics, and 罔兩 / liminal-shadow themes."]
  > — zh.wikipedia.org/zh-tw 洪凌 (作家). *Secondary.* Note: prior recon called the prize "全球華語科幻獎 Merit Award" — Wikipedia gives the canonical Chinese name as **全球華人科幻小說獎** and the level as **優選** (often translated Honorable Mention rather than Merit). Story collections 《肢解異獸》(*Dismembering Exotic Beasts*) and 《異端吸血鬼列傳》 (*The Heretical Vampire Chronicles*), both 1995, established her as a queer / cyberpunk / posthumanist voice. Multiple Taiwan academic theses devoted specifically to her (e.g. 中央大學 2007 thesis "酷兒與科幻——洪凌小說初探(1995-2005)"; 中興大學 thesis on "台灣九○年代酷兒科幻小說中的後人類政治"). Her position pairs queer/SF in a way that no mainland figure does — and predates the 2010s "new wave." Hong Ling alone is reason enough to add a Taiwan slot.

Less central but worth flagging:
- **徐曉晴 (Xú Xiǎoqíng)**: Crown Award winner for 《抉擇》.
- **文旦 (Wén Dàn)**: 2000 Crown Award for 《二四俱樂部》.
- The cross-Strait category ambiguity: Taiwan publishers (聯經, 印刻, 木馬) have published mainland women's SF (e.g. 郝景芳's 《孤獨深處》). Anthology-level cross-pollination is real; author-level crossing is rare.

### 1.3 Hong Kong

**The honest finding: Hong Kong genre-SF women's tradition is thin to nonexistent in the surfaced record.** The dominant Sinophone HK SF tradition is **倪匡 (Ní Kuāng)**'s 衛斯理 *Wesley* series (1963–2004) — a single male-author franchise so dominant it almost defines what "HK SF" means in publishing terms. The 鄭政恆 (Cheng Cheng-hang) survey in 《明報月刊》 2019 (the most authoritative source-language overview of Sinophone SF history) treats HK women's SF essentially as absent: it names no HK women genre-SF writers.

What the HK literary tradition has instead is **speculative-literary women writers** whose work *contains* SF/speculative elements without being marketed as genre SF:
- **西西 (Xī Xī, 1937–2022)** — 《飛氈》 (*Flying Carpet*, 1996) is part of her 肥土鎮 cycle; magical-historical / speculative; engages science, technology, ecology in fragments rather than as core genre. Frequently cited as an SF-adjacent figure but resists the category. Considered for inclusion would be a stretch but defensible.
- **韓麗珠 (Hón Lai-chu / Hán Lìzhū, b. 1978)** — 《縫身》 (*Sewn*, 2010) and 《空臉》 (*Faceless*, 2017) are speculative-dystopian works on bodies and law (compulsory marriage-by-stitching legislation; mandatory facial standardisation). 鄭政恆 reads them as the strongest HK women's-speculative-fiction case. Stylistically closer to Han Kang or Yoko Tawada than to Liu Cixin. **Defensible Phase-2 inclusion under the dataset's existing tolerance for speculative-literary work.**

  > *excerpt pending — zh.wikipedia.org/zh-tw 韓麗珠 entry fetched this session lists 《縫身》 and 《空臉》 in the bibliography but contains no thematic description beyond "作品風格帶有超現實主義色彩" (her work carries surrealist colouring). The dystopian-bodies-and-law framing comes from 鄭政恆's 明報月刊 piece, which was not re-fetched this session and which now becomes the load-bearing source for HK inclusion.*
- **黃碧雲 (Wong Bik-wan, b. 1961)** — primarily literary; speculative threads occasional rather than structural. Probably out of scope.

The structural absence in HK genre-SF women's writing is itself a finding — almost certainly a function of (a) the 倪匡 monopoly absorbing the genre's commercial oxygen for decades, (b) HK's small and Cantonese-vernacular literary market favouring serial / newspaper / film over novel-form genre fiction, and (c) the absence of a HK equivalent to 《科幻世界》 as institutional anchor.

## 2. Author candidates for Phase 2

Generationally grouped. One-line "why" each. **Already in dataset (do not redo): 郝景芳, 夏笳, 迟卉, 糖匪.**

### 2.1 Predecessor generation (1980s–early 1990s)

- **晶静 / 张静 (Jīng Jìng / Zhāng Jìng)** — 1986 银河奖 winner for 《神秘的声波》, the first woman to receive the prize; 1985 《最美的眼睛》 in 科学文艺 may be the earliest published PRC women's SF in the reform era. Mythological-SF corpus (女娲, 织女, 夸父, 盘古). Written about the era retrospectively in 《"灰姑娘"华丽转身前后》 (科普创作评论, 2019). **First-priority Phase-2 add — anchors the predecessor generation.**
- **張曉風 (Zhāng Xiǎofēng)** — 1968 《潘渡娜》, the foundational Sinophone women's-SF text. Primarily essayist; SF appearances are sparse but historically pivotal. **Tier-1 add for periodisation reasons; without her, the chronology lies.**
- **凌晨 (Líng Chén)** — debut 1995 (《信使》, 银河奖), Beijing-based, aerospace-family background. Multiple 银河奖. **Has actual novels** — 《月球背面》 (*The Far Side of the Moon*) and 《睡豚，醒来》 (*Wake, Sleeping Dolphin*, 2017, AI-themed). This directly addresses the `gaps.md` claim that Chinese women's novel-length SF is sparse — **凌晨 has a novel-length corpus that the dataset has missed.** Tier-1 add.

  > *excerpt pending — Baidu Baike URL https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%87%8C%E6%99%A8/2816 returned HTTP 404 this session. The slug is wrong; the correct disambiguation parenthetical for the SF writer (科幻作家 / 作家) needs to be tried. Phase 2 should fetch via search-redirect, not direct URL.*
- **赵海虹 (Zhào Hǎihóng, b. 1977, Hangzhou)** — debut 1996 (《升成》), 1997 银河奖 for 《桦树的眼睛》, multiple later wins. PhD in Art History (中国美术学院, 2012). Often called "中国科幻界的公主" — a moniker worth noting, not necessarily endorsing. Works include 《伊俄卡斯达》 (mythology/gender), 《时间的彼方》, 《水晶的天空》. **陈楸帆 specifically names her as a strong female-perspective writer in his 2018 《光明日报》 essay (work cited: 《伊俄卡斯达》).** Tier-1 add.

### 2.2 Mid-generation (2000s–2010s, contemporaries of the four already covered)

- **钱莉芳 (Qián Lìfāng, b. 1978, Wuxi)** — secondary-school history teacher. 《天意》 (*Destiny*, 2004) sold 150,000 copies, was the SF bestseller for seven consecutive years, won the 银河奖 special prize. Liu Cixin has explicitly credited *Tianyi* as the success that gave him confidence to write 《三体》 — **a documented influence statement going from a woman writer to Liu Cixin, which would be a notable edge in the dataset.** Historical-SF / counterfactual-Han-dynasty mode. Tier-1 add.

  > *excerpt pending — Baidu Baike URL for 《天意》 (https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%A4%A9%E6%84%8F/96570) returned HTTP 404 this session. The Liu Cixin attribution remains unsourced verbatim and must be confirmed before being recorded as a primary-source influence edge. Phase 2 must locate the original interview / preface.*
- **顾适 (Gù Shì)** — already considered/dropped in Phase 1 per `research-log.md`. **Senior urban planner**; XPRIZE SFAC member. 《嵌合体》 (2016 星云金奖, 2017 Hugo longlist), 《莫比乌斯时空》 (2017 银河奖最佳短篇, full collection 2020 八光分). Engages "女性主义" explicitly in critical reception ("不失女性主义的温暖辉光"). The drop in Phase 1 should be revisited; she fits the dataset cleanly.
- **程婧波 (Chéng Jìngbō)** — already mentioned. Editor of 《她》 anthology (2023); 《倒悬的天空》 collection; 2023 星云短篇金奖 for 《且放白鹿》, 2020 32nd 银河奖 中篇 for 《去他的时间尽头》. Both writer and field-shaper. **Tier-1 add.**
- **王侃瑜 (Wáng Kǎnyú / Regina Kanyu Wang, b. 1990)** — already in dataset's research-log as "editorial role." This undersells her: bilingual writer, 复旦 MFA, 多次 星云奖 winner (best SF film concept gold; best 中篇 silver for 《云雾》). Two collections: 《云雾2.2》 (2018), 《海鲜饭店》. Currently Oslo postdoc. **The dataset's framing should be corrected — she is a writer who also edits, not the reverse.** Tier-1 add.
- **彭柳蓉 (Péng Liǔróng)** — 《控虫师》 (*Bug Controller*, 2017) is a million-selling YA-leaning SF novel, with associated webdrama / mobile-game adaptations. Engages a large reader market that prize-circuit accounts ignore. **Important precisely because she's outside the 《科幻世界》 gatekeeping.** Tier-2 add.
- **吴霜 (Wú Shuāng)** — appears in Huxiu round-up alongside 王侃瑜 etc.; needs verification of corpus.

### 2.3 Newer voices (post-2018, post-Hugo era)

- **双翅目 (Shuāngchìmù, "Diptera")** — pen name. PhD-trained in philosophy. Story collection includes 《公鸡王子》 (*The Rooster Prince*); engages information-theoretic and philosophical-SF modes. Star of the 《不存在》 / 八光分 axis rather than 《科幻世界》. Tier-1 add.
- **慕明 (Mù Míng)** — software-engineering background. Works: 《铸梦》, 《沙与星》, 《宛转环》, 《蓬尘录》, 《刍狗》 — the latter three engage classical Chinese narrative traditions / Daoist references in SF frame. Specifically discussed in 《对谈科幻作家慕明、双翅目》 (机核, 2024). Tier-1 add.

## 3. What is structurally invisible

**Pre-1980s women's SF in PRC: essentially zero.** The 1950s–early-1970s 童恩正 / 郑文光 / 叶永烈 generation is uniformly male in surfaced records. This is real, not a sourcing artefact: the political conditions for SF publishing under Mao excluded most women writers from the science-popularisation infrastructure that produced 1950s–60s SF. The first identifiable PRC woman SF writer is 张静 in 1985–86. The 17-year gap (1949–1966) and the Cultural Revolution decade (1966–1976) close off a tradition that may have existed in unpublished form but did not enter print. **This absence should be recorded as historical, not as a sourcing gap.**

**Web-novel SF on 晋江 (Jinjiang) and 起点 (Qidian).** 晋江 is the dominant women-readers' platform; 起点 is the dominant men-readers' platform. The split is institutional: 晋江 hosts massive volumes of speculative fiction by women, but the platform's classification system files them as 言情 (romance), 仙侠 (xianxia / immortal heroes), 玄幻 (xuanhuan / Chinese-mythos fantasy), 穿越 (time-travel / transmigration) — almost never as 科幻. The 中国作家网 2022 piece 《女孩们的"叙世诗"》 documents that 2020–2021 saw 晋江 women writers explicitly engaging "后人类" (posthuman) and high-concept worldbuilding inside 仙侠 / 无CP frames, but the genre-policing line still reads these as fantasy/romance. Print-prize circuits (银河奖 / 星云奖) systematically ignore this corpus. **This is the largest hidden body of women's speculative writing in Chinese — possibly tens of thousands of authors — and it does not enter the SF canon by design.** A serious Phase-2 dataset should at minimum acknowledge this; full inclusion would dwarf the print-tracked roster.

**The 言情 / 玄幻 / 仙侠 / 科幻 boundary as a gendered policing mechanism.** Mainland critical discourse routinely treats 仙侠 as not-SF and 科幻 as the serious form. The boundary maps roughly onto the 起点 / 晋江 split, which maps roughly onto male / female readerships. **The genre line cuts against women writers more than men.** A man writing speculative fiction at scale is more likely to be processed as 科幻; a woman writing speculative fiction at scale is more likely to be processed as 玄幻 or 仙侠 and excluded from the SF canon. This is documentable in the asymmetry between 起点 and 晋江's category structures. The dataset's "what counts as SF" question is unusually live for the Chinese case.

**Hong Kong genre-SF women's tradition.** As noted in 1.3, this is essentially absent — and the absence is structural (倪匡 monopoly, market size, market language). The HK women writers we can identify (西西, 韓麗珠, 黃碧雲) are speculative-literary, not genre-SF. **Treat the HK gap as a real historical absence, not a research failure.** This contradicts an implicit assumption in `gaps.md` that women's SF is everywhere if you look hard enough; in Cantophone HK genre publishing, it isn't.

**Mid-1990s mainland women SF cohort.** Between 张静 (1986) and the 凌晨 / 赵海虹 debut window (1995–1997), there is a cohort that exists in the 银河奖 records but has rarely been written about. The 知乎 银河奖 历届 list is the best Chinese-language source; English secondary literature contains essentially nothing on this period. Phase-2 should pull the full 银河奖 winners list cross-referenced for women names year-by-year.

## 4. Sourcing-quality notes

**What worked:**
- **Baidu Baike** is reliable for biographical baselines (birth year, debut, prize record) for established figures. Confirmed against secondary refs in this session and held up.
- **Zhihu (知乎) long-form posts** ("历年历届中国科幻银河奖获奖作品大全" etc.) are useful aggregators that surface names absent from English coverage. Treat as secondary-aggregator, not primary.
- **中国作家网 (chinawriter.com.cn)** runs the most consistent literary-establishment criticism on SF in Chinese, including gender-themed pieces (《建构中国科幻文学的女性话语空间》, 张悦, 文艺报 2021-12-29). Reliable for citations of authors and works; the ideological frame is establishment-cultural-policy and should be read as such.
- **澎湃新闻 (thepaper.cn)** carried the 90s-cohort interview series and the "科幻的边缘，边缘的科幻" roundtable. Strong for contemporary author voices in their own words.
- **科普创作评论 (Science Writing Review)** published 张静's first-person retrospective on the 1980s 科学文艺 / 银河奖 era — primary-source-grade material from a participant.
- **Douban book pages** confirm publication dates, ISBN, edition history; useful for translation-history work.
- **PanSci Asia** (繁體中文) for Taiwan-side critical context.
- **明報月刊 / Mingpao Monthly** for HK / Sinophone overview (鄭政恆's 華文科幻小說史 is the single most authoritative source-language overview of the field's structure).

**What did not work:**
- **CNKI (中国知网) abstracts**: not directly fetched this session — paywall behaviour expected; would need institutional login. Phase 2 should budget a librarian assist.
- **The Fudan 《东方学刊》 PDF on six periods of Chinese SF** (the most authoritative academic periodisation source) returned corrupted binary via WebFetch. The contents matter for the dataset's framing of Chinese SF history; Phase 2 needs an alternative access path.
- **Zhihu deep-thread answers** (`zhuanlan.zhihu.com` 历届银河奖 list, the question-page on women SF authors): both returned HTTP 403 from WebFetch this session. Search summaries surfaced the headline content but not the full lists. A different fetch method or a manual visit is needed for the complete 银河奖-by-year roster.
- **WeChat-public-account pieces**: in-app, often referenced, not externally crawlable. The strongest-looking critical pieces on the 不存在 platform are unreadable from outside the WeChat ecosystem.
- **科幻世界 official site (sfw.com.cn)** has the 历届 winners list but the page surfaced as a URL only; full retrieval may require direct browser access or the magazine archive at CDSTM.

**Encyclopedia error patterns spotted in passing:** English-language Wikipedia and SFE on Chinese women SF authors typically lag mainland Wikipedia by 2–4 years on awards and publications; pinyin tone marks are inconsistent; pen names sometimes elided. Mainland zh.wikipedia.org generally has better author bibliographies than en.wikipedia.org for this cohort. For Taiwan / HK figures the situation reverses for some authors.

**One direct contradiction with `gaps.md`'s framing worth surfacing:** the gaps file states that "Chinese women's SF novel-length work" is "extremely sparse" and that "novels by Chinese women SF writers in English translation are extremely sparse" — these are not the same claim. The first is empirically wrong in source language: 凌晨, 钱莉芳, 彭柳蓉 (and 郝景芳 already in the dataset) all have novel-length work in Chinese. The second — sparseness in English translation — is correct. The dataset's gap is a translation-pipeline gap, not a writing gap. Phase 2 should restate this distinction.
