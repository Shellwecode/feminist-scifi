# Reconnaissance: Japanese Women's SF (Japanese-language sources)

A source-language survey of the field's shape — infrastructure, candidates, structural absences, sourcing reliability — conducted to inform Phase 2 expansion beyond the four Japanese authors currently in the dataset (鈴木いづみ / Suzuki Izumi, 大原まり子 / Ōhara Mariko, 倉橋由美子 / Kurahashi Yumiko, 小川洋子 / Ogawa Yoko). The memo follows the source priority rule: Japanese-language sources first, English secondary only as confirmation. Original-script titles in the body (*アマノン国往還記*) with rōmaji + English gloss in parens on first mention.

> **Corrections from prior recon / dataset (logged during this session):**
> - **`gaps.md`-style framing of Japanese SF as "thin pre-2020 in English criticism" understates the source-language density.** Japanese SF has had a formal feminist-SF critical apparatus since 1994 (小谷真理 *女性状無意識* / *Joseijō Muishiki* / *Techno-Gynesis*, awarded the 15th 日本SF大賞 Special Prize that year). The "thin pre-2020" claim is true only of English-language reception of *Japanese* feminist SF; the field-internal apparatus is roughly thirty years old.
> - **The dataset's four-author Japanese roster has a structural omission rather than a name error.** All four entries are prose-novel writers. The single most consequential foundational figure in Japanese women's SF — **萩尾望都 (Hagio Moto)** — is a manga creator whose *バルバラ異界* won the 27th 日本SF大賞 (2006), placing manga inside the official SF canon at the highest institutional level. The dataset's prose-only filter excludes the foundational woman in the canon-internal record.
> - **The Phase-1 rejection of 多和田葉子 (Tawada Yōko) as "cross-category, watchlist" stands.** Japanese-language Wikipedia and SF Magazine reception do not categorise *献灯使* (*Kentōshi*, 2014) primarily as SF; she is consistently positioned in literary-establishment frames (Akutagawa 1993, Tanizaki 2003). The dystopian/speculative reading is plausible and not contested but is not the field-internal anchor. Stays on watchlist.
> - **The Phase-1 rejection of 新井素子 (Arai Motoko) "for space" should be reconsidered.** Arai is the foundational woman in postwar Japanese genre-SF: 1977 debut at age 16 in the 1st 奇想天外 SF new-writer prize honourable mention; 12th and 13th 星雲賞 (1981, 1982); 20th 日本SF大賞 (1999) for *チグリスとユーフラテス*. She is the institutional anchor figure for women in the Japanese SF Writers Club genealogy. **Tier-1 omission.**

## 1. Field infrastructure

### 1.1 Magazines

The Japanese SF magazine ecology is unusual among the surveyed regions for its longevity, depth, and the explicit role of women editors and women-themed special issues.

- ***SFマガジン*** (*S-F Magazine*, 早川書房 / Hayakawa Shobō, 1959–present) — the field's flagship and the single longest-running SF magazine in any of the six recons. Originally licenced from *F&SF*; subsequently developed its own original-fiction line. The "**女性作家特集**" (women writers special issues) are a recurring feature; verify specific issue numbers in Phase 2.
- ***奇想天外*** (*Kisō Tengai*, 1974–1981) — the second-anchor magazine of the 1970s. Ran the 奇想天外SF新人賞 (Kisō Tengai SF New Writer Award) whose 1st edition (1977) gave 新井素子 her honourable-mention debut at age 16. The 奇想天外 fold in 1981 marks the end of the magazine boom and the start of the *SFマガジン* monoculture.
- ***NW-SF*** (New Wave SF, 1970–1982, ed. 山野浩一) — small-press avant-garde venue; surfaced fewer women writers than the mainstream pipeline.
- ***別冊宝石 SF特集号*** (Bessatsu Hōseki, 1956 onward) — first-generation SF anthology issues; pre-magazine-era.
- ***SFアドベンチャー*** (徳間書店, 1979–1993) — second-tier mass-SF magazine.
- ***SF Japan*** (徳間書店, 1999–2010) — replacement venue.
- ***ナイトランド・クォータリー*** (Night Land Quarterly, 2010s–) — speculative/horror crossover.

### 1.2 Awards

- ***日本SF大賞*** (Nihon SF Taishō, **Japan SF Grand Prize**, 1980+, administered by **日本SF作家クラブ** / Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of Japan). The single most authoritative prize. Women winners in the primary award:
    - 第15回 (1994) **小谷真理** — *女性状無意識 (Techno-Gynesis)* — **Special Prize** (功績賞 / 特別賞 framing varies). Critic-author entry; foundational feminist-SF apparatus.
    - 第18回 (1997) **宮部みゆき** — *蒲生邸事件* (*The Gamō Mansion Incident*).
    - 第20回 (1999) **新井素子** — *チグリスとユーフラテス* (*Tigris and Euphrates*).
    - 第27回 (2006) **萩尾望都** — *バルバラ異界* (*Barbara Otherworld*). **Manga.**
    - 第37回 (2016) **白井弓子** — *WOMBS（ウームズ）*. **Manga.**
    - 第41回 (2020) **菅浩江** — *歓喜の歌 博物館惑星III* (*Ode to Joy: Museum Planet III*). (Source disagrees with the brief's *永遠の森* attribution — verify in Phase 2; the prize-year is 第41回 / 2020 per ja.wikipedia.org Suga Hiroe entry.)
    - 第45回 (2024) **市川春子** — *宝石の国* (*Land of the Lustrous*). **Manga.**

  **Three of seven women winners are manga creators.** This is structurally unique across the six recons surveyed and forces the manga-SF inclusion question.

- ***星雲賞*** (Seiun Award, 1970+, fan-voted at 日本SF大会). Women laureates in the Japanese categories (selective):
    - 第12回 (1981) **新井素子** — *グリーン・レクイエム* (Green Requiem) — 日本短編.
    - 第13回 (1982) **新井素子** — *ネプチューン* — 日本短編.
    - 第21回 (1990) **大原まり子** — *アクアプラネット* (Aqua Planet) — 日本短編.
    - 第24回 (1993) **菅浩江** — *そばかすのフィギュア* — 日本短編.
    - 第39回 (2008) **有川浩 (有川ひろ)** — *図書館戦争* シリーズ — 日本長編.
    - 第50回 (2019), 第51回 (2020) **菅浩江** — *暗黒声優*, *不見の月* — 日本短編.
    - Comic category includes CLAMP (2001, *カードキャプターさくら*) and the Hagio Moto manga prizes (1980, 1983 — *スター・レッド*, *銀の三角*).

  Hagio Moto received the 11th 星雲賞 (1980) Comic for *スター・レッド* and the 14th (1983) for *銀の三角* — confirming her presence in the SF prize circuit two decades before the 日本SF大賞 win.

- ***ハヤカワSFコンテスト*** (Hayakawa SF Contest, 2013+) — current new-writer pipeline at 早川書房. Recent cohort runs through this prize; verify women shortlisted year-by-year in Phase 2.
- ***創元SF短編賞*** (Sōgen SF Short Fiction Award, 東京創元社, 2010+) — short-form pipeline. **高山羽根子** (Takayama Haneko) won an early edition; subsequently 芥川賞 2020 for *首里の馬* — a 創元SF短編賞 → 芥川賞 trajectory of the type that complicates genre-SF classification.
- ***小松左京賞*** (Komatsu Sakyō Award, 角川春樹事務所, 1999–c. 2009) — short-lived novel-length prize.
- ***センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞*** (Sense of Gender Award, **2001+**, administered by **ジェンダーSF研究会** founded by 小谷真理 with 巽孝之 and others in **2000**) — the single most important explicitly feminist-SF prize in any of the six recons. **菅浩江** *誰に見しょとて* won the 13th edition (2014, Grand Prize) — a primary-source citation that her work has been read as gender-themed SF by the dedicated feminist-SF jury. Phase 2 should pull the full winners roster.

### 1.3 Anthologies (canon-forming)

- ***年刊日本SF傑作選*** (*Annual Japanese SF Best-Of*, 大森望 + 日下三蔵 ed., 創元SF文庫, 2008–present). Year-by-year canon. Verify women contributor counts.
- ***NOVA*** anthology series (大森望 ed., 河出文庫, 2009–2014; revived as *NOVA 2019*+). Contemporary original-anthology spine.
- ***日本SF精神史*** / ***日本SF全集*** (出版芸術社) — multivolume canon-formation projects.
- ***伊藤典夫翻訳SF傑作選*** and ***浅倉久志アンソロジー*** — translator-anchored anthologies that index canon by translation history.
- **The "women-only" anthology corresponding to *《她》* (China 2023) or *Universo Desconstruído* (Brazil 2013) does not surface in this session's search.** This is itself a finding: the canon-formation-event pattern identified in synthesis §1 has **not** taken the women-only-anthology form in Japan. The feminist-SF apparatus is instead institution-shaped — ジェンダーSF研究会, センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞, 小谷真理's books — rather than anthology-shaped.

### 1.4 Presses

- **早川書房 (Hayakawa Shobō)** — the flagship; runs *SFマガジン*, the ハヤカワSFコンテスト, and most translation imprints.
- **東京創元社 (Tokyo Sōgensha)** — second-anchor; runs 創元SF短編賞 and the 年刊日本SF傑作選 series.
- **河出書房新社 (Kawade Shobō Shinsha)** — *NOVA* anthology home; broader literary-SF crossover.
- **徳間書店 (Tokuma Shoten)** — historical SF imprint; SFアドベンチャー / SF Japan magazine home.
- **角川書店 / 角川春樹事務所 (Kadokawa)** — broad genre publishing; 小松左京賞 home.
- **集英社 (Shūeisha) / 小学館 (Shōgakukan) / 講談社 (Kōdansha)** — manga publishers carrying the shōjo-SF tradition (Hagio Moto's 別冊少女コミック home; *11人いる！* serialised in 小学館 1975).
- **国書刊行会 (Kokushokankōkai)** — small-press SF, Yamao Yūko's later home.
- **書肆侃侃房 / 早川文庫JA / 講談社タイガ** — newer imprints.

### 1.5 Academic and critical infrastructure

- **巽孝之 (Tatsumi Takayuki)** — emeritus, Keio University Department of English. The dominant Japanese SF critic of his generation; bilingual; author of *Full Metal Apache* (Duke UP, 2006). His Japanese-language *日本SF論争史* (勁草書房 2000) is canonical secondary work for postwar Japanese SF history.
- **小谷真理 (Kotani Mari)** — independent critic, married to 巽孝之 (1987). **The single most important figure in Japanese feminist-SF infrastructure.** Founded the **ジェンダーSF研究会** (Gender SF Research Association, 2000) and through it the センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞 (2001+). Major works:
    - *女性状無意識 — テクノガイネーシス、女性SF論序説* (1994, 勁草書房) — won the 15th 日本SF大賞 special prize 1994. **The founding text of Japanese feminist-SF criticism.**
    - *聖母エヴァンゲリオン* (1997, マガジンハウス) — feminist reading of the Evangelion phenomenon.
    - *ファンタジーの冒険* (1998), *エイリアン・ベッドフェロウズ* (2004), *テクノゴシック* (2005), *ジェンダーSF研究* series.
    - Co-translator with 巽孝之 of *Cyborg Feminism* — BABEL International Translation Award (1991).
    - Former chair of 日本ペンクラブ女性作家委員会 (Japan PEN Club Women Writers Committee, 2003); judge for the 日本ファンタジーノベル大賞 (from 2002); SFWJ member.

  > 「『女性状無意識―テクノガイネーシス、女性SF論序説』(1994年) で第15回日本SF大賞特別賞を受賞」
  >
  > [translated from Japanese: original / English: "*Female-state Unconscious — Techno-Gynesis, an Introduction to Women's SF Theory* (1994) received the 15th Japan SF Grand Prize Special Award."]
  > — ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/小谷真理. *Secondary (encyclopedia).* Confirms the 1994 institutional ratification — the feminist-SF apparatus is field-internal-recognised, not marginal.
- **新島進 (Niijima Susumu)** — academic critic; SF translation criticism.
- **長澤唯史 (Nagasawa Tadashi)** — university critic with SF specialisation.
- **岡和田晃 (Okawada Akira)** — SF / fantasy criticism, editorial work.
- **大森望 (Ōmori Nozomi)** — critic-anthologist-translator; the dominant gatekeeper-curator of contemporary Japanese SF, edits *年刊日本SF傑作選* and *NOVA*; reviews in *SFマガジン*. Methodologically not feminist-inflected; his editorial selections set the canon.
- **新城カズマ (Shinjō Kazuma)** — writer-critic of light-novel SF; useful for the LN pipeline question.
- **海老原豊 (Ebihara Yutaka)** — younger academic, SF criticism.
- **Universities:** Keio (巽 lineage), Tokyo, Kyoto, ICU, Waseda. **早稲田大学SF研究会** is the most consequential university SF circle historically.

### 1.6 Online / community infrastructure

- **日本SF作家クラブ (SFWJ / Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of Japan)** — sfwj.jp. Administers 日本SF大賞. Membership-based; 小谷真理 is a member, 新井素子 served as President (2014–2018).
- **日本SF大会** — annual SF convention; fan-voted 星雲賞 announced there.
- **SFセミナー** — annual SF criticism convention.
- **ジェンダーSF研究会** — gender-sf.org. 小谷真理's flagship organisation.
- **SFファンジン大賞** — fanzine-circuit prize.
- **note / 小説家になろう / カクヨム / エブリスタ** — web-novel platforms. *小説家になろう* (*Narō*) is the dominant 異世界転生 (isekai) male-coded platform; *エブリスタ* skews more female-coded; *カクヨム* (KADOKAWA, 2016) is genre-mixed and has piped into the print-light-novel canon.

## 2. Author candidates by generation

Generationally grouped. **Already in dataset (do not redo): 鈴木いづみ, 大原まり子, 倉橋由美子, 小川洋子.** Confidence tiers: **Well-documented** / **Suspected** / **Rumored**.

### 2.1 Pre-1970s — the structural absence

**No women SF writer is identifiable in the postwar Japanese SF first generation (c. 1957–1970).** The first-generation pipeline (*SFマガジン* 1959+, *宇宙塵* fan-press 1957+) is uniformly male in the surfaced canon: 星新一, 小松左京, 筒井康隆, 光瀬龍, 半村良, 眉村卓, 平井和正, 山田正紀. The Hayakawa / 早川 1960s editorial roster contains no woman SF writer of equivalent stature.

**The 1960s women's-literary corpus that contained speculative work was filed under "literature," not SF.** 倉橋由美子 (already in dataset) is the clearest example: *パルタイ* (1960), *スミヤキストQの冒険* (1969), *アマノン国往還記* (1986) are speculative-dystopian but were positioned by 河出 / 新潮 as literary fiction. This is the Japanese instance of synthesis §1's "foundational women filed as literary" pattern.

### 2.2 1970s "First Wave" — manga-SF as the women's-SF entry point

**The structurally distinctive Japanese pattern:** while the prose-SF first generation excluded women, the **少女漫画 (shōjo manga)** tradition produced a parallel SF canon in the early 1970s anchored by women creators. This is the regional pattern that no other recon has surfaced.

- **萩尾望都 (Hagio Moto, b. 1949)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented.** Foundational figure. *11人いる！* (*Jūichinin Iru!* / *They Were Eleven*) serialised in *別冊少女コミック* (*Bessatsu Shōjo Comic*) **September–November 1975** at Shōgakukan; **the first full-scale SF work in shōjo manga**. Subsequent SF works: *スター・レッド* (1978–79, won 11th 星雲賞 Comic 1980), *銀の三角* (1980–82, won 14th 星雲賞 Comic 1983), *マージナル* (1985–87), *バルバラ異界* (2002–05, **27th 日本SF大賞 2006**). The 2006 SFWJ award is the institutional act that ratifies manga as inside the SF canon at the highest level. **Excluding her from the dataset is a structural omission of the foundational woman in canon-internal Japanese SF history.**

  > 「少女マンガ初の本格的なSF作品」
  >
  > [translated from Japanese: original / English: "the first full-scale SF work in shōjo manga"]
  > — ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/11人いる！. *Secondary (encyclopedia).* Confirms the foundational positioning. The same article notes its readership extended beyond typical shōjo audiences to "小説家・文化人" (novelists and cultural figures), and that it won the 21st 小学館漫画賞 (Shōgakukan Manga Award, 少年少女部門) for 1975.
- **竹宮惠子 (Takemiya Keiko, b. 1950)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented (manga).** *地球へ…* (*Toward the Terra*, 1977–80, 月刊マンガ少年). 第26回小学館漫画賞 (1980). Far-future post-human / esper-SF in the shōjo-manga frame. Animated 1980 and 2007. With Hagio, half of the **24年組** (Year 24 Group) of women shōjo manga creators whose work systematically engaged SF / gender / sexuality themes.
- **佐藤史生 (Satō Shio, 1952–2010)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented (manga).** *夢みる惑星* (1980–84), *ワン・ゼロ* (1984–86) — hard-SF / cyber-SF in the shōjo frame; less anglophone-recognised than Hagio but cited by 小谷真理 and field-internal sources as a major figure.
- **山岸凉子 (Yamagishi Ryōko, b. 1947)** — **Tier-2, Well-documented (manga).** *処天 (日出処の天子)* primarily historical; speculative threads occasional. Borderline SF inclusion.
- **栗本薫 (Kurimoto Kaoru, 1953–2009)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented (prose, but cross-medium).** *グイン・サーガ* (1979–2009, 130+ vols) is heroic fantasy; her SF includes *時の石* and other Hayakawa-published shorts. Massive bibliographic footprint; categorical SF affinity is partial.
- **山尾悠子 (Yamao Yūko, b. 1955)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented (prose).** Debut 1975 in *SFマガジン* (高2 in 山形); collections *仮面舞踏会* (1977), *夢の棲む街* (1982). Long retirement; comeback in the 2000s with *飛ぶ孔雀* (2018, 泉鏡花文学賞 + 日本SF大賞 38th candidate; verify exact prize record). Fantastical-SF more than hard-SF, but the field-internal critical record (大森望, 巽孝之) anchors her as SF. **Phase-1 omission worth correcting.**

### 2.3 1980s — Arai Motoko and the 奇想天外 cohort

- **新井素子 (Arai Motoko, b. 1960-08-08, Tokyo-Nerima)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented.** Debut 1977 at 16 with *あたしの中の……* (*Atashi no Naka no……*) in the 1st 奇想天外SF新人賞 (Kisō Tengai SF New Writer Award) honourable mention, judged by 星新一, 小松左京, 筒井康隆. 12th 星雲賞 短編 1981 (*グリーン・レクイエム*), 13th 星雲賞 短編 1982 (*ネプチューン*), 20th 日本SF大賞 1999 (*チグリスとユーフラテス*). SFWJ President 2014–2018. Rikkyō University, German Literature. Married 1985. **Foundational woman in postwar Japanese genre-SF prose; the dataset's omission of her is the single most consequential gap.** Field-internal reception describes her as "a pioneering figure in light novels" developing a "新口語体" (new colloquial prose) — a stylistic innovation that the older male SFWJ generation (Komatsu, Tsutsui) initially objected to per the ja.wikipedia.org entry.
- **氷室冴子 (Himuro Saeko, 1957–2008)** — **Tier-1 (light-novel-adjacent SF).** Cobalt Bunko mainstay; *なぎさボーイ*, *クララ白書* (school-SF crossover), *銀の海 金の大地* (historical-fantasy). With 新井素子 the foundational figure of the 1980s women's-coded SF / light-novel pipeline. SF categorisation partial.
- **久美沙織 (Kumi Saori, b. 1959)** — Tier-2. Light-novel / SF cross; *ドラゴンクエスト* novelisations and original SF.
- **皆川博子 (Minagawa Hiroko, b. 1929)** — **Tier-2.** Mystery-fantastic-historical; speculative work occasional rather than central.
- **倉橋由美子 (already in dataset)** — *アマノン国往還記* (1986) places her at the explicit feminist-SF apex. The 1986 publication aligns with the broader 1980s women's-SF wave even though Kurahashi's career trajectory was older.
- **鈴木いづみ (already in dataset, d. 1986)** — career ends with her death in the year *アマノン国往還記* publishes; 2021 Verso *Terminal Boredom* English translation is the recovery event.

### 2.4 1990s "Cyberpunk-era / Second Wave"

- **大原まり子 (already in dataset)** — *ハイブリッド・チャイルド* (1990, 早川), 21st 星雲賞 1990 short. The institutional anchor of 1990s women's prose SF.
- **菅浩江 (Suga Hiroe, b. 1963-04-21, Kyoto)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented.** 1981 debut *ブルー・フライト* in *SF宝石* (recommended by 矢野徹). *メルサスの少年* (1992, 23rd 星雲賞 日本長編); *永遠の森 博物館惑星* (2000, **32nd 星雲賞 日本長編 + 第54回 日本推理作家協会賞**); 24th 星雲賞 短編 1993 (*そばかすのフィギュア*); 50th and 51st 星雲賞 短編 2019, 2020 (*暗黒声優*, *不見の月*); **13th センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞 Grand Prize 2014 (*誰に見しょとて*)** — the primary-source citation that her work has been read as gender-themed SF by the dedicated feminist-SF jury; **41st 日本SF大賞 2020 (*歓喜の歌 博物館惑星III*)**. **Tier-1 add; the most decorated woman SF prose writer in the 1990s–2020s Japanese canon after 新井素子.** Note: the project brief attributed her 日本SF大賞 win to *永遠の森* (2000); ja.wikipedia.org assigns the 2000 prize to the 32nd 星雲賞 + 日本推理作家協会賞, and the 41st 日本SF大賞 to the third *博物館惑星* volume *歓喜の歌* (2020). Brief misattribution corrected.
- **岬兄悟** (male, exclude).
- **新井素子** — continues through the 1990s.
- **小谷真理** — *女性状無意識* (1994) and *聖母エヴァンゲリオン* (1997) anchor the critical apparatus. **Tier-1 candidate as critic-with-field-shaping-role, parallel to 程婧波 / Ana Rüsche / Grace Dillon in the cross-recon pattern.** The dataset's tolerance for critic-authors (cf. Brazilian Ana Rüsche) covers her.

### 2.5 2000s–2010s — literary-speculative

- **小川洋子 (already in dataset)** — anchors the literary-speculative end.
- **桜庭一樹 (Sakuraba Kazuki, b. 1971)** — **Tier-2.** *赤朽葉家の伝説* (2006, 日本推理作家協会賞), 直木賞 2007. Genre-crossing; SF affinity intermittent. Inclusion debatable.
- **恒川光太郎 (male, exclude).**
- **皆川博子** — continues.
- **長野まゆみ (Nagano Mayumi, b. 1959)** — Tier-3. Speculative-literary; SF affinity light.
- **村田沙耶香 (Murata Sayaka, b. 1979-08-14, Chiba)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented.** *授乳* / *Silver Song* (2008), *消滅世界* (*Vanishing World*, 2015), *殺人出産* (*Murder Birth*, 2014), *地球星人* (*Earthlings*, 2018), *コンビニ人間* (*Convenience Store Woman*, 2016, **155th 芥川賞**). Strong Phase-2 add.** Japanese-language Wikipedia does *not* explicitly frame her work as SF; the SF / speculative-dystopia framing comes primarily from Anglophone reception (Takemori translations, NYRB, etc.). ### 2.6 Post-2018 — the Hayakawa SF Contest pipeline and 創元 short cohort

- **高山羽根子 (Takayama Haneko, b. 1975-05-09, Toyama)** — **Tier-1, Well-documented.** 1st 創元SF短編賞 佳作 / 佳作賞 (*うどん キツネつきの*, **2009 inaugural edition** — the recon's earlier "2010" date was approximate; ja.wikipedia.org gives 2009); novels *オブジェクタム* (2018), *居た場所* (2019), *Come Gather Round People* (2019); **163rd 芥川賞 2020 for *首里の馬***. Won 林芙美子文学賞 (Hayashi Fumiko Literary Prize) in 2016. **創元SF短編賞 → 芥川賞 trajectory** — the type-specimen of the genre-to-literary boundary crossing.

## 3. Categories of invisibility

### 3.1 Manga-SF — the structural category question

**The single most important Japan-specific finding.** Three of seven women winners of the 日本SF大賞 are manga creators (萩尾望都 2006, 白井弓子 2016, 市川春子 2024). The field-internal canon explicitly ratifies manga as SF at the highest institutional level. No other surveyed tradition has this pattern — Chinese, Brazilian, Korean, MENA, South Asian, and Indigenous SF all operate predominantly in prose.

This forces a `primary_medium` question the other regions do not raise. **The dataset's current prose-only filter excludes the foundational woman in Japanese SF (萩尾望都) and at least three additional 日本SF大賞 / 星雲賞 women laureates.** Excluding them is defensible only by a strict prose-only inclusion rule; the field's own canon does not respect that rule.

**Flag this explicitly as a category question rather than presenting it as a settled inclusion.** Decisions to make:
1. Whether the dataset's `primary_medium` field accepts "manga" as a value at parity with "prose."
2. Whether manga-SF inclusion is gated on field-internal SF-canon recognition (i.e. 日本SF大賞 / 星雲賞 award presence) rather than on the dataset researcher's prose preference.
3. Whether the 24年組 (Year 24 Group) shōjo-manga generation (萩尾, 竹宮, 山岸, 大島弓子) is treated as a unit — they are a documented cohort with feminist-SF themes (gender-fluid bodies, queer relationships, post-human futures).

The Phase-2 recommendation is to extend the Tidbeck-precedent-style inclusion logic to manga-SF: any creator with a 日本SF大賞 or 星雲賞 SF-category win is eligible, with an in-file `primary_medium` flag.

### 3.2 Light-novel / web-novel women's-SF

**The Japanese web-novel pipeline has a gendered platform split parallel to the Chinese 晋江/起点 case but less severe.** *小説家になろう* (Narō) is male-coded, dominated by 異世界転生 (isekai), and produces the bulk of light-novel SF. *エブリスタ* and *Berry's Café* skew more female-coded. *カクヨム* (KADOKAWA) is genre-mixed. The print-light-novel pipeline (電撃文庫, 富士見ファンタジア文庫, MF文庫J, ガガガ文庫) has women writers, but they are systematically under-counted in the 日本SF大賞 / 星雲賞 prize circuit.

Recurring women LN writers: **桑島由一**, **時雨沢恵一** (male), **谷川流** (male, exclude); the actual women cohort surfaces less in surfaced searches and warrants a Phase-2 dedicated pass. **氷室冴子** (1957–2008) was the foundational woman of the Cobalt Bunko light-novel-adjacent SF generation.

The pattern is the same as the Chinese / Korean case: women writing at scale, classified outside the SF canon by the platform's genre-policing line.

### 3.3 Anime / SF screenwriter women

The major animation-SF auteurs are male (押井守, 富野由悠季, 庵野秀明, 今敏). Women SF screenwriters surface less in surfaced sources. **大河内一楼** (male). **佐藤大** (male). **岡田麿里 (Okada Mari)** is a major woman scenarist but works mainly in romance / coming-of-age rather than SF. **吉田玲子** — children's / animation including some SF. The screenwriter pipeline parallels the prose-SF first generation in being male-dominated; the women-of-anime-SF question warrants its own Phase-2 pass but does not appear to have a Hagio-equivalent figure.

### 3.4 Pre-1970s women in Japanese SF

**Largely absent — the foundational figures are uniformly male.** 倉橋由美子's *パルタイ* (1960) is the earliest dataset-relevant work and is positioned literary, not SF. The 1960s 早川 *SFマガジン* pipeline did not surface women writers in this session's search. Verify in Phase 2 against 巽孝之 *日本SF論争史* (2000) — there may be 1960s women contributors to *SFマガジン* under male pen names or in single-story appearances. The absence is most likely structural (the postwar Japanese SF first generation was infrastructurally male, drawing from science-fan and *宇宙塵* fanzine networks that were male-coded) rather than a sourcing artefact, but verification is warranted.

### 3.5 Cross-language Japanese-diaspora writers

**多和田葉子** is the dominant figure (Japanese-German, writes in both). **柳美里** (Korean-Japanese, ethnic-Korean Japan-born; *JR上野駅公園口* National Book Award 2020) is literary, not SF. **温又柔** (Taiwanese-Japanese) is literary. The cross-language SF axis in Japanese has essentially one figure (Tawada), unlike the Chinese case where multiple bilingual writers operate.

### 3.6 The "women-only anthology" non-event

**No woman-edited women-only SF anthology of the *《她》* / *Universo Desconstruído* / *Love after the End* type has surfaced for Japanese SF in this session.** The feminist-SF infrastructural moves have been institutional (ジェンダーSF研究会 2000, センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞 2001+) and critical (小谷真理 *女性状無意識* 1994) rather than anthology-shaped. **This breaks the synthesis §1 canon-formation-event pattern for Japan.**

The pattern divergence is itself the finding: the Japanese feminist-SF apparatus has a thirty-year-old prize, a thirty-year-old research association, and a thirty-year-old critical-theoretical book — but it does not have the women-only canon-formation anthology. The institutional substitute (a prize that selects gender-themed SF regardless of author gender) may have absorbed the canon-formation function. **Phase 2 should verify whether a women-only Japanese SF anthology exists somewhere unsurfaced before concluding this is structurally true.**

## 4. What surprised you

**The manga-SF inclusion question is the structurally largest finding.** No other recon surfaced a tradition where the foundational woman in the official SF canon (萩尾望都, 27th 日本SF大賞 2006) is a manga creator. Three of seven women 日本SF大賞 winners are manga creators. This is a category question, not a marginal one — and it is the one place where the dataset's prose-default filter actively excludes the canon-internal record.

**The feminist-SF critical apparatus in Japan is older than I expected and predates the 1990s English-language feminist-SF apparatus by very little.** 小谷真理 *女性状無意識* (1994, won 日本SF大賞 special prize 1994) is contemporaneous with Constance Penley's *NASA/Trek* (1997), Donna Haraway's *Modest_Witness* (1997), and the consolidation of feminist-SF criticism in English. The "thin pre-2020 in English" framing of *research-log.md* captures the English-reception gap but understates the source-language density — by 1994 Japan had a 日本SF大賞-recognised feminist-SF book, by 2000 it had a Gender SF Research Association, by 2001 a Sense of Gender Award. The English-language reception is what is thin; the field itself is not.

**Arai Motoko's omission from the dataset is harder to defend than the brief implied.** "Omitted for space" — but she debuted at 16 in 1977, won the 12th and 13th 星雲賞 short, won the 20th 日本SF大賞 (1999), and served as President of SFWJ 2014–2018. She is the institutional anchor figure for women in Japanese genre-SF prose, and her absence is the most consequential single omission alongside Hagio Moto.

**The synthesis §1 canon-formation-event pattern does not hold for Japan.** Where China has *《她》* (2023), Brazil has *Universo Desconstruído* (2013), Indigenous has *Walking the Clouds* (2012), Japan has — institutions (ジェンダーSF研究会, センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞) and a single critical text (小谷真理 *女性状無意識* 1994). The pattern's absence is informative: Japan's feminist-SF infrastructure consolidated through institutional / prize / academic forms rather than anthology forms, and it consolidated earlier (mid-1990s) than the other recons' canon-formation events (2010s–2020s).

**The Phase-1 attribution that 倉橋由美子's clearest feminist SF (*アマノン国往還記*) is "not in English" is correct and load-bearing for the dataset's translation gap.** It is published 1986, has been continuously in print in Japanese (河出文庫 reprints), and as of this session has no English translation. The dataset's existing 倉橋由美子 entry should retain this framing explicitly.

## 5. Sourcing-quality notes

**What worked:**
- **ja.wikipedia.org** is consistently more accurate than en.wikipedia.org for women Japanese SF authors. Bibliography listings, award years, and prize categories match other Japanese-language sources where checked. Author-by-author entries for 萩尾望都, 新井素子, 菅浩江, 小谷真理, 多和田葉子, 大原まり子, 倉橋由美子 are well-maintained.
- **ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/日本SF大賞** and **ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/星雲賞** give complete year-by-year winner rosters. These are the canonical source for award-record claims.
- The **SFWJ official site (sfwj.jp)** is structurally relevant — administers the 日本SF大賞 — but most of its records redirect to ja.wikipedia or to *SFマガジン* features.
- **早川書房 (Hayakawa Shobō) book product pages** are reliable for publication dates and ISBN.
- **The ジェンダーSF研究会 site** is the single primary source for センス・オブ・ジェンダー賞 winners; not exhaustively crawled this session but a Phase-2 priority.

**What didn't work / requires direct access:**
- **ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/11人いる！** required URL-encoding-with-half-width-exclamation: the *full-width* exclamation `！` returned HTTP 404; the *half-width* `!` (URL-encoded as `%21`) succeeded. Phase 2 should default to the half-width form for Japanese title URLs containing exclamation/question marks.
- **小谷真理's own publications** (*女性状無意識*, *聖母エヴァンゲリオン*, etc.) are 勁草書房 / マガジンハウス imprints; tables of contents and chapter summaries were not retrievable from the open web this session.
- **Cinii / 国立国会図書館 NDL catalogue search** is open but full-text not. Phase 2 should pull NDL catalogue entries for 1960s–80s 早川 *SFマガジン* issues to verify the absence/presence of women contributors.
- **The 巽孝之 *日本SF論争史* (2000) and *日本SF精神史* monographs** are not on the open web in full text; library access required.
- **奇想天外 magazine back issues** are out of print (1974–81 run); confirmation of 新井素子's exact 1977 award details would benefit from a back-issue scan, not currently accessible.
- The recurring problem with WeChat content in the China recon has no Japanese parallel — the issue here is print-archival rather than platform-locked.

**Encyclopedia error patterns spotted:** English-language Wikipedia and SFE tend to under-record manga-SF (萩尾望都, 白井弓子, 市川春子) as SF, even though Japanese reception ratifies them as such. SFE's 萩尾望都 entry exists but is light on the 日本SF大賞 record. English-language summaries of Japanese SF (e.g. Anglophone SF Encyclopedia overview essays) often omit 小谷真理 entirely or list her without the 1994 日本SF大賞 special prize, understating her institutional weight.

**One direct contradiction with `research-log.md`'s framing:** the note that "Japanese SF criticism in English is thin pre-2020; the 2021 Verso *Terminal Boredom* translation is the main vehicle for English reception of Suzuki" is accurate as far as it goes — but it elides the source-language critical density. The dataset's Japanese gap is overwhelmingly a translation / reception gap, not a writing or criticism gap. 小谷真理's *女性状無意識* (1994) is foundational and untranslated. 倉橋由美子's *アマノン国往還記* (1986) is the canonical feminist-SF novel and untranslated. Three of seven women 日本SF大賞 winners are manga creators whose work the prose-default dataset frame excludes. **Phase 2 should reframe the Japan gap explicitly as "translation pipeline + medium-filter" rather than "thin pre-2020."**
