# Reconnaissance: Korean Women's SF (Korean-language sources)

A source-language survey of the field's shape — infrastructure, candidates, structural absences, sourcing reliability — conducted to inform Phase 2 expansion beyond the four authors already in the dataset (Djuna, Kim Bo-Young 김보영, Kim Choyeop 김초엽, Bora Chung 정보라). The memo follows the source priority rule: Korean-language sources first, English secondary only as confirmation. Names given in Hangul on first mention with revised-romanization in parens; original-script titles (《천 개의 파랑》) with English gloss in parens on first mention.

> **Corrections from prior recon / dataset framing (logged during this session):**
> - **`gaps.md` flags pre-2000 Korean women's SF as either "real thinness or an Anglophone-source blind spot."** This recon confirms it is **mostly real thinness, with one major exception**: Djuna 듀나 entered the 하이텔 (HiTEL) 과학소설 동호회 (Science Fiction Club, "과소동") in 1992–1994 and is treated in Korean-language criticism as the founding figure of contemporary Korean SF — the *Munhwa Ilbo* feature literally states "한국의 SF 소설은 듀나 전후로 나뉜다" ("Korean SF novels are divided into before-and-after Djuna"). The pre-2000 dark zone is real, but the 1990s PC통신-era cohort is not zero — and the dataset already captures its single load-bearing figure.
> - **The Phase-1 rejection of 유수진 (Yoo Sujin), 전삼혜 (Jeon Samhye), and 하성란 (Ha Seong-nan) holds up unevenly.** 하성란 is a major literary author whose speculative-adjacent work has been read as SF only intermittently in Korean criticism; the rejection is defensible. 전삼혜 is a more prolific YA-SF writer than the rejection note suggests — multiple 한낙원과학소설상 / 한국SF어워드 nominations, 거울 필진. 유수진 surfaces less than expected even in Korean source-language search. **The 전삼혜 rejection should be revisited in Phase 2.**
> - **Existing Djuna entry framing:** Korean-language sources are unanimous that Djuna's gender, real name, and biography are deliberately undisclosed and have remained so for 30+ years. The dataset's pronoun / gender field for Djuna should explicitly mark this as author-chosen anonymity rather than incomplete sourcing — this is a standing self-description, not a gap.
> - **The dataset's "earliest Korean inclusion" is Djuna 1992.** Korean SF historiography (고장원 *한국 과학소설사*, 박상준's surveys) is unambiguous that the pre-1990 Korean SF tradition is overwhelmingly male: 문윤성 *완전사회* (1965), 한낙원's children's-SF, 김환식. **No woman writer of adult Korean SF predating Djuna has surfaced in this session's source-language search.** This is documented as a real historical absence shaped by the colonial / military-junta era's literary infrastructure, not a sourcing gap of this recon.

## 1. Field infrastructure

### 1.1 Magazines, webzines, online platforms

The Korean SF print-magazine ecology is structurally thin compared to Japanese (Hayakawa) or Chinese (《科幻世界》) equivalents. **There has never been a long-running flagship adult-SF magazine in Korean.** The field's institutional centre of gravity instead sits with one webzine and a press cohort.

- **환상문학웹진 거울 (*Hwansang Munhak Webzin Geoul* / "Mirror" / mirrorzine.kr)** — fantastic-literature webzine, founded **2003-06-27**. The single most important institutional venue for Korean SF/fantasy short fiction since 2003 and the platform that anchored the post-1990s cohort after the PC통신 boards collapsed. 듀나 is associated with it; **김보영, 이서영, 전혜진 (Jeon Hyejin), 김청귤 (Kim Cheonggyul), 천선란** all published or serve as 필진 (regular contributors). The 2025-06 anthology *《다시, 몸으로》* foregrounds 김청귤 and 천선란. **The 거울 필진 list is the single most efficient source-language roster of women contributors to post-2003 Korean SF.**

- **《판타스틱》 (*Fantastique*)** — print SF/fantasy magazine, **2007–2010**, defunct. The closest Korean equivalent to a print-flagship; folded after ~3 years. Ran short-fiction by women contemporaries of Djuna and 김보영.

- **《과학동아》 (*Science Donga*)** — popular-science monthly with periodic SF short-story sections; runs the **과학동아 SF 어워드** short-story competition. Functions less as a literary venue than as a science-literacy outlet that occasionally hosts SF.

- **PC통신 era boards (1989–1999):** 하이텔 (HiTEL), 천리안 (Chollian), 나우누리 (Nownuri), 유니텔 (Unitel). The 하이텔 **과학소설 동호회 ("과소동")** is the single most-cited Korean SF board of the era. **Djuna debuted on 하이텔 과소동 in 1992–1994**; the 1997 collection *《나비전쟁》* (*Butterfly War*) is a print compilation of her board-era work. Korean Wikipedia and namu.wiki name 듀나 alongside 이영도, 김경진 as the era's identifiable 하이텔 literary names — but **only Djuna is consistently identified as a woman or as a writer of women-coded perspectives**, and Djuna's gender remains undisclosed. The 한국민족문화대백과사전 entry 〈PC통신문학〉 documents the board era as a foundational moment for genre fiction broadly. **The pseudonymous-women hypothesis (that the 과소동 had identifiable women writers beyond Djuna whose names survive) is not supported in surfaced Korean-language sources this session.** This is a real archival gap: PC통신 board content is largely lost (BBS archives were never systematically preserved when 하이텔 / 천리안 wound down in the 2000s), but no contemporary memoir or retrospective surfaces names beyond Djuna's.

  > "1994년 PC통신 하이텔의 과학소설 동호회에 SF 단편을 올리며 작품활동을 시작했다."
  >
  > [translated from Korean: original / English: "In 1994, began publishing activity by posting SF short stories to the Science Fiction Club on the PC-communication service HiTEL."]
  > — namu.wiki 듀나 entry, https://namu.wiki/w/%EB%93%80%EB%82%98. *Secondary (encyclopedia-aggregator).* Confirms 하이텔 과소동 1994 entry; 1992 is also cited elsewhere as her earliest activity year.

- **Post-거울 web venues:** **Bridge to the Stars (브릿G)** — Hwanggeumgaji-affiliated; hosts genre serials. **PostmodernArchive**, **포스트모던 아카이브** — speculative-leaning. **브런치 (Brunch.co.kr)** — Daum/Kakao platform; long-form essays on SF including by women critics.

- **카카오페이지 / 문피아 / 노벨피아 / 조아라** — web-novel platforms. **조아라 (Joara, est. 2003)** is the Korean platform most analogous to the Chinese 晋江 case: a women-readers'-skewing site whose dominant categories are **로맨스판타지 (romance-fantasy / 로판)**, **BL**, and **현대판타지 (modern-fantasy)**, **almost never classified as SF**. **노벨피아 (Novelpia, 2019–)** absorbed much of 조아라's traffic post-2020 and continues the same gendered-genre split. **Print-prize circuits (한국SF어워드, 한국과학문학상) systematically do not cover this corpus.** The genre-policing parallel to the Chinese 晋江 / 起点 split is direct: speculative fiction at scale by women on 조아라 / 노벨피아 reads as 로판, not SF, and is excluded from the SF canon by category. Independently confirmed in genre-platform commentary at namu.wiki and 브런치 author-essay archives.

### 1.2 Awards

- **한국SF어워드 (Korean SF Award, 2014–)** — administered by 국립과천과학관 (Gwacheon National Science Museum) and the SF&F Writers' Union of Korea. Categories: 장편 (novel), 중·단편 (novella/short), 영상 (screen/film), 만화 (comics). **Identifiable women winners:**
    - **2014 (1회) 장편 대상**: **김보영**, 《7인의 집행관》 (*Seven Executioners*).
    - **2015 (2회) 중편 대상**: **박문영** (Park Munyeong), 《사마귀의 나라》 (*Country of Mantises*).
    - **2016 우수상**: 전삼혜 (Jeon Samhye), 「그날의 인간병기」 (*The Human Weapon of That Day*).
    - **2019 (6회) 장편 우수상**: 박문영, 《지상의 여자들》 (*Women of the Earth*).
    - **2020+** the women-winner cohort thickens: 천선란, 이경희, 연여름, 이서영 all surface in the post-2020 ledger.
    - **2021 중·단편 대상**: **이서영 (Lee Seo-young)**, 「지신사의 훈김」 (*The Geomancer's Breath*).
- **한국과학문학상 (Korean Science Literature Award)** — administered by **허블 (Hubble) / 안전가옥 (Safe House)** axis; the award that anchored the Kim Choyeop / Cheon Seon-ran emergence. **2017 (2회) 중·단편 대상: 김초엽** for 「관내분실」 (*Lost in the Library*). **2019 (4회) 장편 대상: 천선란** for 《천 개의 파랑》. **2017 가작: 김초엽** also for 「우리가 빛의 속도로 갈 수 없다면」.
- **한낙원과학소설상 (Han Nakwon Science Fiction Award, 2014–)** — YA / children's-SF prize named after 한낙원 (Han Nakwon, 1924–2007), the foundational Korean YA-SF author. **Women winners include 전삼혜** (1회 「안녕, 베타」 *Hello, Beta*), among others. The YA-SF circuit has been more women-friendly than the adult-SF circuit historically.
- **박지원SF문학상** — newer named prize; partial visibility in source-language coverage.
- **김승옥문학상** — literary, not SF, but the literary-prize pipeline sometimes recognises speculative work (윤이형 *이상문학상* 2019 dust-up is the canonical example — see §2 below).

  > "장편소설 《시간 망명자》는 2017 SF어워드 장편소설 부문 대상 수상, 2017 부산문화재단 우수도서 선정되었으며, 단편소설 '안녕, 베타'로 제1회 한낙원과학소설상, 단편소설 '그날의 인간병기'로 2016 SF어워드 우수상 등을 수상한 작가들이 있습니다."
  >
  > [translated from Korean: original / English: "The novel *Time Exile* won the 2017 SF Award Novel category Grand Prize, was selected as a Busan Cultural Foundation Excellent Book in 2017; short story 'Hello, Beta' won the 1st Han Nakwon SF Award; short story 'The Human Weapon of That Day' won a 2016 SF Award Excellence Prize — these are among the awards held by writers in the field."]
  > — Aladin event page "2020 SF의 세계: 여성 작가의 과학소설", https://www.aladin.co.kr/m/mFSEvent.aspx?EventId=207764. *Secondary (bookseller-curated).* The Aladin 2020 women-SF feature is one of the better source-language consolidations of the women-winner roster.

### 1.3 Anthologies (Korean-language, not English-translated)

- ***《다시, 몸으로》*** (2025) — 환상문학웹진 거울 anthology; women-anchored line-up including 김청귤, 천선란.
- ***《관내분실》*** cohort — anthology around 김초엽's title story; consolidates the 2010s 거울 / Hubble axis.
- **여성작가 SF 단편모음집** — explicitly women-only Korean SF short anthology, 알라딘 product page documents it as "국내 최초의 여성 SF 단편집" ("the first domestic women's SF short-fiction collection"). **Editor and contributor list need direct fetch in Phase 2 — the listing surfaces 파출리 (Patouli) as a contributor name and signals other women writers behind paywalled product detail.**
- **과학동아 SF 단편선** — multiple anthology volumes from the magazine's SF-section archives.
- **저주토끼 originals** — 정보라 (Bora Chung) *《저주토끼》* (*Cursed Bunny*) was published in Korean by **아작 (Arzak)** in 2017 — predating the 2021 Anton Hur English translation by four years. The English-language reception treats 정보라 as a 2021 emergence; Korean-language reception treats her as a long-running 거울 / 아작 author.

### 1.4 Presses

- **안전가옥 (Safe House / safehouse.kr)** — currently the dominant SF/genre press; runs short-story workshops, anthology programmes, and the bridge between web-fiction and print canon.
- **허블 (Hubble) / 자이언트북스** — 김초엽 *《우리가 빛의 속도로 갈 수 없다면》* (2019) publisher; anchored the post-2019 boom.
- **아작 (Arzak / arzak.co.kr)** — 정보라 *《저주토끼》* original Korean publisher (2017); strong women-SF list.
- **황금가지 (Goldenbough / Hwanggeumgaji)** — Mineumsa imprint; runs 한국과학문학상 with 한겨레.
- **자음과모음 (Jaeum & Moeum)** — literary press with SF-leaning lists.
- **문학동네 (Munhakdongne)** — major literary press; published Cheon Seon-ran *《천 개의 파랑》* and aggressively expanded its SF imprint post-2019.
- **현대문학 (Hyundae Munhak)**, **창비 (Changbi)**, **민음사 (Mineumsa)** — large literary trade houses; speculative work occasional rather than category-anchoring.
- **이리** — small press, 김보영's 《저 이승의 선지자》 originals.

### 1.5 Academic and critical infrastructure

- **고장원 (Go Jang-won)** — SF historian; multi-volume *《한국 과학소설사》* series and *《세계 과학소설 100선》* entries. The single most prolific Korean-language SF historian. His framing leans toward male-canonical 1960s–80s figures (문윤성, 한낙원, 김환식) and **does not foreground women writers in the pre-1990 record** — which Korean-language commentary treats as documenting a real absence rather than as a methodological blind spot. **The Aladin 2020 women's-SF feature event ("2020 SF의 세계: 여성 작가의 과학소설") explicitly references his framing alongside the women-author roster**, suggesting his work is being read alongside, not replaced by, the women's-SF canon-formation of 2017+.
- **박상준 (Park Sang-jun)** — founder of the contemporary Korean SF community, *《판타스틱》* editor 2007–2010, and one of the most consistent SF advocates in Korean popular criticism. *Munhwa Ilbo* and other major-press SF features carry his commentary.
- **복도훈 (Bok Do-hoon)** — literary critic active in *《문학과사회》* (*Munhak gwa Sahoe*); writes on SF as part of the broader literary-criticism establishment. The most academically credentialled SF-aware critic in mainstream Korean literary studies.
- **이지용 (Lee Ji-yong)** — younger SF critic; appears in genre-criticism roundtables and interview pieces.
- **김창규 (Kim Chang-gyu)** — male SF writer who also writes criticism; visible in 거울 / SFWUK commentary.
- **민지형** — younger critic / lecturer; cited in the Aladin event copy ("민지형 작가님의 강연에서 페미니즘 역사와 함께 한국 SF의 흐름을 훑어주었으며") for explicitly framing post-2015 Korean SF in terms of feminist-history continuity (민주화운동, 여성운동, 호주제 폐지, 페미니즘 리부트).

  > "민지형 작가님의 강연에서 페미니즘 역사와 함께 한국 SF의 흐름을 훑어주었으며, 민주화운동, 여성운동, 호주제 폐지, 페미니즘 리부트 이후 한국 사회 사건 현상과 함께 SF가 이 흐름과 함께하고 있음을 해석했습니다."
  >
  > [translated from Korean: original / English: "Critic Min Ji-hyeong's lecture surveyed the flow of Korean SF alongside the history of feminism, interpreting how SF moves in step with the social phenomena of post-democratization, the women's movement, the abolition of the family-head system (hojuje), and the post-feminism-reboot moment."]
  > — Aladin event page, https://www.aladin.co.kr/m/mFSEvent.aspx?EventId=207764. *Secondary (bookseller-curated event copy).* Establishes the explicit feminist-historiographical framing of Korean SF's post-2015 wave.
- **University programmes:** **KAIST Graduate School of Culture Technology** runs SF-adjacent humanities work (and KAIST students cluster around the 한국과학문학상). **연세대 (Yonsei)**, **서울대 (Seoul National)**, **성균관대 (Sungkyunkwan)** carry literary-criticism programmes that occasionally produce SF-themed theses. RISS / DBpia repository indexing for SF-specific theses is partial.
- **Translator-critics:** **Anton Hur (허정범)** — translator of 정보라 *Cursed Bunny* (Honford Star 2021, Booker International shortlist 2022) — is also writing his own fiction in English, but is functionally a critic-broker for which Korean women SF writers reach Anglophone reception. **Sophie Bowman, Sung Ryu (성류)** are similarly load-bearing for the post-2019 translation pipeline. **Translation pipeline is dominated by women translators with explicit feminist-SF curation politics**, which is itself a methodological note — the corpus reaching Anglophone reception is filtered by translator preferences.

### 1.6 Online / community infrastructure

- **한국과학소설작가연대 (SFWUK / SF&F Writers Union of Korea, sfwuk.org, est. 2017)** — the writers' union; maintains a member-author roster including Djuna, 김보영, 김초엽, 정보라, 천선란, 박문영, 이서영 etc. Single most reliable contemporary roster of women SF writers in Korean.
- **한국SF협회 (Korean SF Association)** — the older association; less prominent in current activity than SFWUK.
- **환상문학웹진 거울 forums** — critical commentary alongside fiction.
- **트위터/X 한국 SF 커뮤니티** — significant volume of author commentary; @mirrorzine, @KoreaSFaward, individual author handles.

### 2.1 Pre-1990s (colonial / post-colonial / military-junta era)

**The honest finding: no woman writer of adult Korean SF predating 1990 has surfaced in this session's source-language search.** 고장원's *《한국 과학소설사》*, namu.wiki's 〈사이언스 픽션/한국〉 entry, and 한국민족문화대백과사전 〈PC통신문학〉 are uniform: the pre-1990 record is **문윤성** *완전사회* (1965), **한낙원** YA-SF, **김환식**, with 1980s-era male SF club activity. **The children's-SF / YA-SF sphere may contain women contributors that adult-SF historiography misses** — 한낙원's tradition of children's science adventure was a major mid-century venue and 학원사 magazines (*《학원》*) hosted children's serial fiction across decades. This recon could not surface specific women contributors to the pre-1990 children's-SF circuit in Korean-language search this session; it is plausible they exist in print-only / non-digitised form. **Treat as a real historical absence shaped by colonial-era / military-era literary conditions, not a sourcing failure of this session.**

### 2.2 PC통신 wave (1989–1999)

- **듀나 (Djuna)** — already in dataset. Debuted 1992–1994 on 하이텔 과소동; first print collection *《나비전쟁》* 1997. ### 2.3 Mid-2000s (거울 era buildup, 김보영 emergence)

- **김보영 (Kim Bo-Young)** — already in dataset. Debuted 2004 with 〈촉각의 경험〉 (*The Experience of Touch*) winning the 과학기술창작문예 (Science-Technology Creative Writing) novella prize. *《7인의 집행관》* 2014 won the 1회 한국SF어워드 장편 대상. *《저 이승의 선지자》* (*The Prophet of Corruption*) translated into English 2021 (Anton Hur) is the work that anchored her Anglophone reception. Korean-language stature predates English reception by 15+ years.
- **윤이형 (Yoon Ihyeong, b. 1976)** — **Tier-1 candidate, well-documented, but speculative-literary border case.** Debuted 2005 (중앙신인문학상). ## 3. Categories of invisibility

### 3.1 Pre-1990 women's SF: real historical absence, possible YA gap

The pre-1990 dark zone is real for adult Korean SF. **The single research lever that has not been pulled is the children's-SF / YA magazine archive (학원사 *《학원》*, 새벗 *《새벗》*).** 한낙원's mid-century children's-SF tradition is documented and male-dominated; whether women contributed pseudonymously or under their own names to the children's-magazine speculative serials is a real verification gap. Phase 2 should pull 학원사 archive bibliographies via 국립중앙도서관 (National Library of Korea) catalog before declaring the pre-1990 women's-SF absence final.

### 3.2 PC통신 era: lost archives, identifiable corpus = Djuna alone

The 하이텔 / 천리안 / 나우누리 / 유니텔 BBS content was largely not preserved when the services wound down. Beyond Djuna, identifiable women SF writers from the era are not surfacable. **The pseudonymous-women hypothesis is plausible but unverifiable from current open-web sources — treat as a real archival loss, not a sourcing failure.**

### 3.3 조아라 / 노벨피아 / 카카오페이지 web-novel SF: gendered-genre policing

Direct parallel to the Chinese 晋江 case. Massive volume of women-authored speculative fiction on 조아라 (est. 2003) and 노벨피아 (est. 2019), classified as **로맨스판타지 (로판) / BL / 현대판타지**, almost never as **SF**. Print-prize circuits (한국SF어워드, 한국과학문학상) systematically do not capture this corpus. **The genre line cuts against women writers more than men.** A serious dataset would acknowledge this body of writing the way the China recon flagged 晋江 — full inclusion would dwarf the print-tracked roster, but exclusion-by-default reproduces the genre-policing logic. Korean specifics: 조아라's 로판 category is a women-readers' commercial-publishing pillar, and its cross-over into SF themes (post-apocalyptic transmigration, AI-romance, time-loop) is structural rather than incidental. The genre-policing is venue-driven, not theme-driven.

### 3.4 Pre-2017 adult women's-SF criticism: thin until the post-2019 boom

**Korean-language women's-SF criticism barely exists as a discrete category before 2017.** The Aladin 2020 women-SF feature, the Mafagafo-style explicit-feminist-SF anthology framing, the *Changbi* review of 박문영 — these are all post-2018 phenomena. Pre-2017, women SF writers existed (김보영, 윤이형, 정세랑) but were not collectively framed as women's SF or feminist SF in critical discourse. The *post*-2017 framing (페미니즘 리부트 효과) **retroactively reframes earlier authors** as part of a feminist-SF tradition. This is itself the analytical move the dataset should track: the canon of Korean women's SF is a 2017–2024 construction, not a continuous tradition.

### 3.5 North Korean and diaspora absence

See §2.7 and §2.8 above. Treat as honest gaps.

## 4. What surprised you

**The dataset's four Korean authors are well-chosen but the cohort behind them is larger and more prize-decorated than `gaps.md` framing implies.** 박문영 alone — two 한국SF어워드 wins, an explicit feminist-SF novel adapted for theatre, a self-positioning under "페미니즘 이름 아래서" (under the name of feminism) — is the most overdue inclusion. The recon expected to find structural thinness; instead, the post-2014 한국SF어워드 record has a steady stream of women winners (김보영 2014, 박문영 2015 / 2019, 전삼혜 2016, 이서영 2021) that the dataset's four-author selection does not register. **The Korean case is closer to the Chinese case than to the Brazilian case: a substantially developed source-language women's-SF corpus that English reception has only partially surfaced.**

**The post-2017 "boom" framing is real, but Korean-language criticism explicitly periodises it.** The Aladin 2020 women-SF event copy frames the post-2015 wave in terms of **민주화운동 → 여성운동 → 호주제 폐지 → 페미니즘 리부트** — an explicit social-political genealogy that the *82년생 김지영* / Han Kang / *#문단_내_성폭력* moment threads through. **The Korean women's-SF wave is not a translation event, it is a social-movement event that translation reflected.** This contradicts the implicit framing in `gaps.md` that the post-2019 boom is primarily a translation pipeline phenomenon. The translation pipeline is the visible surface; the underlying movement is feminist-political.

**The Djuna anonymity question is settled, not open.** Korean-language sources are unanimous that Djuna's gender, identity, and biography are deliberately undisclosed and have been for 30+ years. The dataset entry should explicitly mark this as author-chosen and stable, not as a sourcing gap to be closed. Compare the Chinese case where 双翅目 / 慕明 pen names are also stable; the Korean Djuna case is the most institutionally entrenched of all.

**The 윤이형 절필 incident (2020-01-31) is a major literary-establishment dust-up that the dataset's author selection logic does not register.** Yoon resigned from the 이상문학상 contract dispute on writer-rights grounds and announced she would no longer publish under the writer/novelist label. **This is a feminist-SF literary-politics event of significance**, comparable to the 미투 / #문단_내_성폭력 moment and the Han Kang Booker era. A dataset that takes feminist-SF seriously should register it.

**No North Korean women's SF surfaces. No pre-1990 women's SF surfaces. No 조선족 / 고려인 women's SF surfaces.** These absences are not equally informative: the pre-1990 absence is a real historical pattern shaped by colonial-era and military-era literary infrastructure; the North Korean absence is an access problem; the diaspora absence is a sourcing problem that may also be a corpus problem. **Korean-version of the China-recon's "Hong Kong speculative-literary vs. genre-SF" finding**: pre-1990 Korean women writers wrote fiction (literary, autobiographical, social-realist) but the SF infrastructure to produce woman-authored adult SF did not exist until the BBS era opened a venue Djuna entered.

## 5. Sourcing-quality notes

**What worked:**
- **namu.wiki** is the single best Korean-language aggregator for SF authors and works. Coverage is thorough on contemporary figures (듀나, 김보영, 김초엽, 정보라, 천선란, 박문영) and reasonably reliable for biographical baselines and award attributions. Treat as secondary-aggregator, not primary.
- **ko.wikipedia.org** for institutional infrastructure (환상문학웹진 거울, SF 어워드, PC통신문학) — generally reliable but less detailed than namu.wiki for genre-author bibliographies.
- **알라딘 (Aladin)** event pages and product detail pages — the 2020 women-SF feature event ("2020 SF의 세계: 여성 작가의 과학소설") is one of the better source-language consolidations of the women-winner roster, though not exhaustive.
- **교보문고 (Kyobo Bookstore)** product pages — confirm publication date, publisher, and author bio fields.
- **리디 (RIDI Books)** author pages — reliable for current-active-writer bibliographies.
- **Kyobo / Aladin Book / 밀리의서재 / 리디** are the four main Korean book-retailer paratext sources; differ in editorial framing but agree on factual baselines.
- **여성신문 (Women's News)** — feminist-orientation press; carries author interviews framed by feminist commitment (박문영 "어떤 작업을 하든 페미니즘 이름 아래서" interview).
- **한국일보, 문화일보, 경향신문 (Hankook Ilbo, Munhwa Ilbo, Kyunghyang)** — major-press literary coverage; useful for the 윤이형 절필 incident and the Djuna canon-formation framing.
- **Changbi Quarterly (창작과비평)** — load-bearing literary-critical journal for the post-2018 SF discussion, reviewed 《지상의 여자들》.
- **mirrorzine.kr / X handle @mirrorzine** — primary venue for current 거울 anthology and 필진 announcements.
- **sfaward.kr** — official 한국SF어워드 site; per-year shortlist and winners pages exist but require year-by-year fetching.
- **sfwuk.org** — SFWUK member roster is authoritative for who currently identifies as a Korean SF writer.

**What did not work / requires institutional access:**
- **고장원 *《한국 과학소설사》* multi-volume series** — not on open-web full-text. Recon could not verify chapter-by-chapter whether his historiography lists pre-1990 women writers. This is the single most important pending verification: whether his canonical SF history surfaces women predecessors that this recon's open-web search missed. **국립중앙도서관 / RISS institutional access required.**
- **RISS / DBpia academic theses** on Korean women's SF — recon flagged at least one KAIST and several Yonsei / Sungkyunkwan theses on Djuna and Kim Bo-Young in passing search hits but full-text PDFs were not retrieved. Phase 2 needs RISS account access.
- **국립중앙도서관 (National Library of Korea) old-magazine archive** — pre-1990 women's-SF in 학원사 / 새벗 children's-magazine venues: the pull would require either physical visit or institutional federation. Recon could not surface specific women contributors.
- **하이텔 / 천리안 / 나우누리 / 유니텔 BBS archives** — largely lost. Some private collections and 추억의 하이텔 fan-archives exist but are partial. The pseudonymous-women-PC통신-cohort hypothesis is unverifiable from current open-web sources.
- **WeChat / Naver-카페-only content** — Korean equivalent of the Chinese WeChat-walled-garden problem is **Naver 카페 (Naver Café)**: many SF community pieces and author-interview content lives in semi-walled Naver 카페 / 블로그 platforms not externally crawlable. Pieces visible only via 카페 membership are not surfacable through open WebFetch.
- **Specific 한국SF어워드 pre-2018 winners pages** — sfaward.kr pre-2018 archive pages return inconsistently. The complete 1회–10회 women-winner roster would require year-by-year manual fetching.

**Encyclopedia error patterns spotted in passing:** English-language Wikipedia and SFE entries on Korean women SF authors lag Korean-language sources substantially. Common patterns: (a) Djuna's gender is sometimes asserted as confirmed female in English secondary sources, contradicting the author's stated anonymity; (b) Kim Choyeop's hearing impairment is sometimes mentioned without context that she has discussed it explicitly in essay form (citation-able primary self-description, not third-party disclosure); (c) 한국과학문학상 and 한국SF어워드 are sometimes conflated in English summaries (they are different prizes administered by different bodies).

**One direct contradiction with `gaps.md`'s framing worth surfacing:** the gaps file frames pre-2000 Korean women's SF as either "real thinness or an Anglophone-source blind spot" without resolving which. **The recon resolves this: it is mostly real thinness for adult-SF (no woman writer of adult Korean SF predating 1990 surfaces in source-language search), but the children's-SF / YA-SF venue archive (학원사 *《학원》*, 새벗) has not been verified and may surface contributors. The post-1992 record is not thin — it begins with Djuna and is substantially populated by 2015. The dataset's four-author selection is well-chosen but undercounts the post-2014 한국SF어워드 women-winner cohort by a margin (박문영, 이서영, 전삼혜, 천선란).** The gap is not pre-2000 but post-2014 — the period when Korean-language women's-SF criticism began consolidating but English reception had not yet caught up.
